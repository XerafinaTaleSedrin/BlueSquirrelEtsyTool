Design Brief: TPS Report Survivor - 1990s Xerox Style

CONCEPT: "TPS REPORT SURVIVOR"
THEME: Vintage Government Document Humor
TARGET AUDIENCE: Government employees, office workers, Gen X/Millennial professionals who remember actual TPS reports and Office Space

DESIGN STYLE: 1990s Xerox/Dot Matrix Aesthetic

TYPOGRAPHY SPECIFICATIONS:
- Main text: "TPS REPORT SURVIVOR"
- Font style: Early dot matrix printer or laser printer aesthetic
- Characteristics: Monospace or near-monospace spacing, slightly pixelated edges
- Reference fonts: Letter Gothic, modified Courier New, or distressed sans-serif
- Text should feel like it came from a government form or official document

VISUAL LAYOUT:
- Main text centered horizontally
- Simple rectangular border around the text (like a government form field)
- Optional elements:
  * Small checkbox with checkmark (☑)
  * Subtitle line: "Certification Date: Every Day" in smaller text
  * Minimal underline or form-style divider

COLOR SPECIFICATIONS:
Primary Version: Black (#000000) on transparent background
Secondary Version: Off-black (#333333) on transparent background
Tertiary Version: Cream (#F5F5DC) on transparent background

DESIGN CONSTRAINTS:
- Must work as a single color design for cost-effective printing
- Transparent background for maximum shirt color flexibility
- Text must be readable at small sizes (down to 3 inches wide)
- Professional enough for workplace wear
- Authentic 1990s government document feel
- Minimal design that matches successful "Currently Clean on OPSEC" aesthetic

PRODUCT APPLICATIONS:
T-Shirt: 4-5 inch width, center chest placement
Hoodie: 5-6 inch width, center chest placement
Mug: 3-4 inch width, centered on front

DELIVERABLES NEEDED:
1. Main design concept with typography treatment
2. Layout specifications (spacing, alignment, proportions)
3. Border/frame design details
4. Scaling guidelines for different products
5. Notes on maintaining authenticity of 1990s office document aesthetic

INSPIRATION REFERENCES:
- 1990s government forms and documents
- Early dot matrix and laser printer output
- Office Space movie aesthetic
- Vintage bureaucratic documentation
- IBM Selectric typewriter output

AVOID:
- Modern, sleek fonts
- Bright colors or gradients
- Complex graphics or illustrations
- Anything that looks too polished or contemporary

Please provide a detailed design concept that captures the authentic feel of surviving endless TPS reports in a 1990s government office environment.
