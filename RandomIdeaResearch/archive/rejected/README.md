# Rejected Ideas Archive

This directory contains research for ideas that received a **No-Go** decision and were not pursued.

---

## Purpose

When you decide not to pursue an idea:
- Move the idea folder here to keep active portfolio clean
- Preserve research and decision reasoning
- Extract lessons to playbook
- Enable future review (ideas may become viable later)

---

## What Goes Here

**Move idea folders here when**:
- Idea has **[No-Go]** status
- Decision is final (not "on hold")
- Want to clear from active ideas
- Research is complete

**Each folder should contain**:
- Original research files (brainstorm, market research, feasibility, decision framework)
- Clear decision reasoning (why it was rejected)
- Lessons extracted for playbook
- Related ideas or pivots to explore

## Learning from Rejected Ideas

Rejected ideas are valuable learning opportunities:

### Immediate Actions After Rejection

1. **Extract to Playbook**: Add specific learnings to playbook files
   - Red flags → `playbook/red-flags.md`
   - Process insights → `playbook/evaluation-insights.md`
   - Market patterns → Update as anti-patterns in `playbook/patterns-that-work.md`

2. **Note Related Ideas**: Document pivots or adjacent opportunities for future exploration

3. **Update Portfolio**: Move entry in `ideas/00_IDEA_PORTFOLIO.md` to "Recently Completed Decisions - Not Pursued"

### Periodic Review

**Monthly**: Review recent rejections
- Are there common patterns in rejection reasons?
- Are you getting faster at identifying red flags?
- Any ideas worth reconsidering?

**Quarterly**: Analyze rejection portfolio
- What % of ideas reach no-go vs. go?
- Most common rejection reasons?
- Average time to no-go decision trending down? (good signal)
- Any rejected ideas now look viable? (market/tech/personal circumstances changed)

**Annually**: Deep review
- Which evaluation criteria predicted rejection accurately?
- How many hours saved by efficient no-go decisions?
- What patterns emerged that should update evaluation framework?

## When to Reconsider Rejected Ideas

Review rejected ideas if:
- **Market shifts**: New regulation, technology, or behavior change
- **Competitive landscape changes**: Dominant player exits or struggles
- **Personal circumstances change**: New skills, resources, or network
- **Technology matures**: Enabling tech becomes viable
- **Time passes**: "Too early" ideas may now be "right time"

**Process for reconsideration**:
1. Review original research and decision
2. Identify what changed since rejection
3. Do quick refresh research (15-30 min)
4. If promising, move back to active ideas and update research
5. If still not viable, update rejection notes

## Value of the Rejected Archive

**This archive is not failure** - it's evidence of:
- Disciplined evaluation process
- Saving time and resources by not pursuing bad ideas
- Learning and pattern recognition
- Strategic selectivity

Every rejected idea makes future evaluations faster and more accurate.

A good "no-go" decision is as valuable as a "go" decision - maybe more valuable because it's harder to make.

---

## Maintaining the Archive

**Keep it clean**:
- Only complete research (don't archive half-researched ideas)
- Ensure decision framework is present
- Extract lessons before archiving

**Make it searchable**:
- Use consistent folder naming
- Include rejection summaries
- Tag common themes in portfolio index

**Use it actively**:
- Reference when researching similar ideas
- Review before starting similar research
- Mine for insights when updating playbook
