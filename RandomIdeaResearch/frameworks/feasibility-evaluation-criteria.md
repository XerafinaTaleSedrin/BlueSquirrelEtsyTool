# Feasibility Evaluation Criteria

**Purpose**: Framework for assessing whether an idea is technically, financially, and operationally viable.

This framework defines how to evaluate if an idea can realistically be built, operated, and monetized successfully.

---

## Evaluation Dimensions

Every idea is scored across **5 dimensions** on a 1-5 scale.

### 1. Technical Feasibility

**Core Question**: Can this be built with available technology and resources?

**Assessment Criteria**:
- Can this be built with existing technology?
- What technical components are required?
- What skills/expertise are needed?
- What's the development complexity?
- What are the technical risks?
- Estimated development timeline?

**Complexity Rating Scale (1-5)**:

**Level 1 (Low Complexity)**:
- No-code tools or existing platforms
- Simple integration or configuration
- Minimal custom development
- Common, well-documented technology
- Examples: Landing page, Airtable-based tool, Zapier automation

**Level 2 (Low-Medium Complexity)**:
- Standard web or mobile app
- Common tech stack (React, Node, Python, etc.)
- Well-established patterns
- Moderate learning curve
- Examples: CRUD app, basic SaaS, simple e-commerce

**Level 3 (Medium Complexity)**:
- Custom development required
- Some specialized technology
- Integration complexity
- Requires specific technical skills
- Examples: Multi-sided marketplace, real-time features, complex workflows

**Level 4 (Medium-High Complexity)**:
- Complex integrations or algorithms
- Specialized or emerging technology
- Performance/scale challenges
- Rare expertise needed
- Examples: AI/ML product, complex data processing, custom hardware integration

**Level 5 (High Complexity)**:
- Novel technology or research-level problems
- Breakthrough innovation required
- Significant technical risk
- Very rare expertise
- Examples: New algorithm development, biotech, advanced robotics

**Technical Feasibility Score**:
- **5/5**: Can build with existing skills and simple tools
- **4/5**: Can build with some learning, common tech stack
- **3/5**: Requires moderate specialized skills or technology
- **2/5**: Requires rare expertise or emerging technology
- **1/5**: Requires breakthrough or currently impossible

**Key Questions**:
- Do I have the technical skills, or can I learn them?
- Is this technically proven or experimental?
- What's the technology risk (dependency on unproven tech)?
- Estimated development time: weeks, months, or years?

### 2. Financial Feasibility

**Core Question**: Can this be built and operated profitably?

#### A. Startup Costs

**Development Costs**:
- Self-build: Time investment (opportunity cost)
- Outsource: Development agency or freelancer costs
- Hybrid: Mix of self and outsourced work

**Initial Infrastructure**:
- Hosting and cloud services setup
- Software licenses and tools
- Domain, branding, design
- Legal setup (LLC, contracts, etc.)

**Launch Budget**:
- Initial marketing/ads
- Landing page and website
- Beta user recruitment
- Testing and iteration

**Total Startup Investment Tiers**:
- **Micro**: <$1K (mostly time, minimal cash outlay)
- **Small**: $1K-$5K (some outsourcing, basic tools)
- **Medium**: $5K-$25K (professional development, marketing)
- **Large**: $25K-$100K (full build, team, marketing)
- **Very Large**: $100K+ (significant capital requirement)

#### B. Ongoing Costs

**Monthly Operating Costs**:
- Infrastructure (hosting, APIs, services)
- Software subscriptions (tools, platforms)
- Marketing and customer acquisition
- Support and maintenance
- Payment processing fees
- Insurance, legal, accounting

**Monthly Burn Rate Tiers**:
- **Low**: <$500/month (can run on side income)
- **Medium**: $500-$2K/month (needs revenue or funding)
- **High**: $2K-$10K/month (requires significant revenue)
- **Very High**: $10K+/month (venture-scale operation)

#### C. Revenue Model

**Monetization Approach**:
- One-time purchase
- Subscription (monthly/annual)
- Transaction fees
- Freemium with upgrades
- Advertising
- Marketplace commission
- Licensing

**Revenue Validation**:
- What do competitors charge?
- What pricing tiers exist in the market?
- Evidence of willingness to pay?
- Estimated customer lifetime value (LTV)
- Estimated customer acquisition cost (CAC)
- LTV/CAC ratio (need 3:1 minimum, ideally 5:1+)

#### D. Financial Viability

**Break-Even Analysis**:
- Monthly costs: $X
- Revenue per customer: $Y
- Customers needed to break even: $X/$Y
- Time to reach break-even: estimate

**Path to Profitability**:
- Clear revenue model?
- Realistic unit economics?
- Achievable customer volume?
- Timeline to profitability?

**Financial Feasibility Score**:
- **5/5**: Low startup cost (<$5K), clear revenue model, fast payback (<6 months)
- **4/5**: Moderate investment ($5K-$25K), proven revenue model, reasonable payback (6-12 months)
- **3/5**: Significant investment ($25K-$100K), viable but unproven model, longer payback (12-24 months)
- **2/5**: High capital need ($100K+), uncertain monetization, long payback (2+ years)
- **1/5**: Prohibitive costs, no clear path to revenue, extremely long or unclear payback

---

### 3. Operational Feasibility

**Core Question**: Can this be operated sustainably?

**Operations Requirements**:

**Daily/Weekly Tasks**:
- Customer support (volume, complexity)
- Content creation or curation
- Inventory management (if applicable)
- Quality assurance
- System monitoring
- Admin tasks

**Monthly/Quarterly Tasks**:
- Feature development and updates
- Marketing and growth activities
- Financial management
- Compliance and legal
- Partnership management
- Strategic planning

**Resource Requirements**:
- Can this be run solo or requires team?
- How many hours per week?
- What specialized skills needed ongoing?
- Can operations be automated?
- What can be outsourced?

**Regulatory/Legal Requirements**:
- Industry-specific regulations
- Licensing requirements
- Data privacy compliance (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)
- Insurance needs
- Legal entity structure
- Contract templates

**Operational Complexity Rating**:

**Low Complexity**:
- Mostly automated
- Minimal ongoing effort (<10 hrs/week)
- No specialized operations needed
- Minimal regulatory requirements
- Examples: SaaS with self-service, digital products, automated services

**Medium Complexity**:
- Regular maintenance required (10-30 hrs/week)
- Some manual processes
- Manageable solo with systems
- Some regulatory compliance
- Examples: Service business, marketplace with support, subscription product

**High Complexity**:
- Constant attention needed (30+ hrs/week)
- Many manual processes
- Likely requires team
- Heavy regulatory burden
- Examples: Agency, complex marketplace, regulated industry service

**Operational Feasibility Score**:
- **5/5**: Highly automated, minimal time, solo-viable, simple compliance
- **4/5**: Regular maintenance, manageable solo, moderate compliance
- **3/5**: Significant time commitment, solo-viable but demanding, notable compliance needs
- **2/5**: Requires team or excessive solo time, complex operations, heavy compliance
- **1/5**: Operationally prohibitive, requires large team, extreme regulatory burden

---

### 4. Market Timing

**Core Question**: Is now the right time for this idea?

**Market Readiness**:
- Is the market ready for this solution?
- Do enabling technologies exist?
- Have customer behaviors shifted to support this?
- Are distribution channels available?

**Trend Analysis**:

**Tailwinds** (favorable trends):
- Growing market
- New enabling technology
- Regulatory changes favoring the idea
- Behavior shifts (remote work, etc.)
- Rising awareness of the problem

**Headwinds** (unfavorable trends):
- Declining market
- Technology becoming obsolete
- Regulatory threats
- Fading trends
- Decreasing problem awareness

**Timing Assessment**:

**Too Early**:
- Market not ready (customers don't understand the need)
- Technology not mature (too many technical risks)
- Distribution channels don't exist
- Education cost is prohibitive

**Right Time**:
- Market awareness growing
- Enabling tech mature enough
- Customer behavior supports adoption
- Clear path to market

**Too Late**:
- Market saturated with solutions
- Consolidation happening
- Winner emerging or already won
- Declining market or trend

**Market Timing Score**:
- **5/5**: Perfect timing - market ready, trends favorable, momentum building
- **4/5**: Good timing - market growing, enabling factors in place
- **3/5**: Neutral timing - market stable, mixed signals
- **2/5**: Poor timing - market declining or too early/late
- **1/5**: Terrible timing - clear market decline or completely wrong time

---

### 5. Personal Fit

**Core Question**: Am I the right person to pursue this?

**Skills & Experience**:
- Do I have relevant skills?
- Have I worked in this industry/domain?
- Do I understand the customer?
- Can I build the technical solution?
- Do I have relevant network/connections?

**Interest & Passion**:
- Am I genuinely interested in this problem?
- Would I still work on this in 2-3 years?
- Does this align with my values?
- Am I excited to talk about this?

**Commitment & Resources**:
- Do I have time to commit? (hours/week available)
- Do I have financial runway? (savings, income, funding)
- Do I have support system? (family, partners, advisors)
- Am I willing to make necessary sacrifices?

**Competitive Advantage**:
- What unique insight or advantage do I have?
- Domain expertise?
- Technical skills?
- Network or distribution?
- Personal brand or reputation?

**Personal Fit Score**:
- **5/5**: Perfect fit - strong skills, deep interest, committed, unique advantage
- **4/5**: Good fit - relevant skills, genuine interest, willing to commit
- **3/5**: Acceptable fit - can learn skills, moderate interest, conditional commitment
- **2/5**: Poor fit - skill gaps, low interest, uncertain commitment
- **1/5**: Bad fit - major skill misalignment, no interest, can't commit

## Risk Assessment

For each idea, identify and rate risks across categories.

### Risk Categories

**Market Risk**: Will customers actually buy this?
- Problem may not be painful enough
- Willingness to pay may be too low
- Market may be too small
- Competition may be too intense

**Technical Risk**: Can this be built as envisioned?
- Technology may not work as expected
- Development may take longer than planned
- Technical dependencies may fail
- Complexity may be underestimated

**Financial Risk**: What if costs exceed estimates?
- Development costs may balloon
- Customer acquisition may be more expensive
- Revenue model may not work
- Burn rate may be unsustainable

**Competitive Risk**: What if competitors respond?
- Incumbents may copy features
- Price wars may emerge
- Better-funded competitor may enter
- Patent or IP issues may arise

**Operational Risk**: Can this be run sustainably?
- Operations may be more complex than expected
- Key person dependency
- Support burden may be overwhelming
- Regulatory compliance may be harder

**Regulatory Risk**: What legal/compliance challenges exist?
- Unclear regulations
- Changing compliance requirements
- Licensing difficulties
- Privacy or security issues

### Risk Rating Scale

**Critical Risk**:
- Deal-breaker if not mitigated
- Could kill the business
- Requires specific mitigation plan before proceeding
- Example: Uncertain legality, dependency on unproven tech

**Significant Risk**:
- Major concern that needs addressing
- Could seriously damage the business
- Requires monitoring and contingency plan
- Example: Strong competitive response, high CAC

**Moderate Risk**:
- Notable concern but manageable
- Normal business risk
- Monitor but don't require special action
- Example: Seasonal demand fluctuation, moderate technical complexity

**Low Risk**:
- Minor concern
- Acceptable within normal business operations
- No special mitigation needed
- Example: Minor regulatory compliance, standard support burden

## Decision Rubric

### Scoring System

Rate each dimension 1-5:
- **5**: Exceptional - Major competitive advantage
- **4**: Strong - Clear viability, no major concerns
- **3**: Adequate - Acceptable but not standout, some concerns
- **2**: Weak - Concerning limitations, significant doubts
- **1**: Critical - Likely fatal flaw, deal-breaker

### Minimum Thresholds for "Go"

**Hard Requirements**:
- ✓ No dimension below 2 (no critical flaws)
- ✓ Average score of 3.0+ across all 5 dimensions
- ✓ At least one dimension rated 4+ (needs a strength)

**Weighted Scoring** (Optional):
Adjust weights based on personal priorities:
- Example: Technical 2x, Financial 1.5x, Personal Fit 1.5x, Operational 1x, Timing 0.5x
- Calculate weighted average

### Interpretation

**Overall Score 4.0-5.0**: Strong idea, high viability
- Clear strengths, minimal concerns
- Strong candidate for pursuit

**Overall Score 3.0-3.9**: Viable idea, moderate confidence
- Acceptable across dimensions
- May require mitigation of specific risks
- Pursue with caution and monitoring

**Overall Score 2.0-2.9**: Weak idea, low confidence
- Significant concerns
- Major risks or limitations
- Likely no-go unless specific factors change

**Overall Score <2.0**: Non-viable idea
- Critical flaws
- Clear no-go
- Archive and move on

## Estimation Guidelines

### Technical Complexity
- Research similar products/projects
- Break down into components
- Estimate each component's difficulty
- Add buffer for unknowns (2x is common)

### Costs
- Get quotes from service providers
- Research competitor pricing for tools
- Check market rates for outsourcing
- Always use ranges, not point estimates
- Add 20-50% buffer

### Timeline
- Estimate based on similar projects
- Account for learning curve
- Factor in part-time vs. full-time
- Add significant buffer (2-3x typical)

### Revenue
- Use competitor pricing as baseline
- Estimate conservative conversion rates (1-5%)
- Model growth scenarios (pessimistic, realistic, optimistic)
- Don't assume hockey-stick growth

## Key Philosophy

**Be ruthlessly realistic**: Optimistic assumptions doom projects. Better to overestimate costs/timeline and underestimate revenue than vice versa.

**Flag assumptions clearly**: Make it explicit what you're assuming vs. what you know.

**Estimate in ranges**: Single-point estimates are always wrong. Use ranges.

**Compare to similar examples**: Don't estimate in a vacuum - research comparable situations.

**Acknowledge uncertainty**: It's okay to not know. Document what you'd need to learn.

## Common Estimation Mistakes

**Technical**:
- ❌ Underestimating learning curve for new technology
- ❌ Forgetting about integration complexity
- ❌ Not accounting for edge cases and bug fixes
- ❌ Assuming third-party APIs will work perfectly

**Financial**:
- ❌ Forgetting ongoing costs (hosting grows with scale)
- ❌ Underestimating customer acquisition cost
- ❌ Overestimating conversion rates
- ❌ Not accounting for payment processing fees, chargebacks

**Operational**:
- ❌ Underestimating support burden
- ❌ Forgetting about compliance and legal ongoing work
- ❌ Not accounting for context-switching between tasks
- ❌ Assuming automation will be easy

**Personal**:
- ❌ Overestimating available time
- ❌ Not accounting for other commitments
- ❌ Assuming interest will stay high
- ❌ Ignoring skill gaps

---

## Next Steps After Feasibility

Based on feasibility analysis:
- **High feasibility (3.5+)** → Proceed to decision framework
- **Medium feasibility (2.5-3.5)** → Identify risks to mitigate, validate key assumptions
- **Low feasibility (<2.5)** → Likely no-go, or requires major pivot/rethinking
- **Critical risks identified** → Define mitigation plan or stop

Feasibility analysis should give you confidence (or clarity on lack thereof) to make a decision.
