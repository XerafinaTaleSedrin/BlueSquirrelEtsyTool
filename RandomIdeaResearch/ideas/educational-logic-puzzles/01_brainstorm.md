## What's the Idea?

Create logic grid puzzles designed to teach factual educational content (history, science, geography, literature). Instead of fictional scenarios, puzzles encode real facts that students must deduce - e.g., "Kings of England" puzzle where students deduce which king reigned when, who their queen was, and key events. Primary market is educators (B2B), with secondary potential as mass-market activity books.

---

## What Problem Does It Solve?

**The Problem**:
Teaching factual content (dates, sequences, associations) is traditionally done through rote memorization, which is boring and has poor retention. Teachers need engaging materials that make students actively process and recall information.

**Who Experiences This Problem?**:
- K-12 teachers (especially history, science, social studies)
- Homeschool parents needing curriculum supplements
- Test prep instructors (SAT, AP, standardized tests)
- Educational content publishers
- Students who struggle with memorization

**How Acute Is the Pain?**:
- [ ] Must-have (people actively search for solutions, willing to pay)
- [x] Should-have (people want it but can work around)
- [ ] Nice-to-have (convenience, not critical)

---

## How Do People Solve It Today?

**Current Solutions**:
1. Traditional worksheets and flashcards - boring, passive recall
2. Educational games (Kahoot, Quizlet) - good for review, not deep learning
3. Mnemonic devices - work for sequences but not complex associations
4. Standard logic puzzles (not educational) - brain training but no curriculum alignment
5. Study guides and textbooks - comprehensive but not engaging

**What's Inadequate About Current Solutions?**:
- Flashcards test recall but don't create meaningful associations
- Kahoot-style games are fun but shallow (multiple choice, speed-based)
- Logic puzzles exist but aren't aligned to educational standards/curriculum
- Teachers spend hours creating custom materials
- Most "educational games" feel like homework in disguise

## Initial Viability Thoughts

**Why This Might Work**:
- Logic puzzles create active recall + association building (evidence-backed for retention)
- Teachers Pay Teachers proves teachers pay for quality time-savers
- Curriculum-aligned content has clear differentiation from generic puzzles
- Already building puzzle generation capability (leverage existing work)
- Activity books are a proven format (low production cost, gift market)
- Museum gift shops as specialized distribution channel (history-themed)

**Why This Might Not Work**:
- Creating curriculum-aligned content requires subject expertise
- Teachers expect free or very cheap resources
- Long sales cycles for school/district purchases
- "Educational puzzles" may feel gimmicky to serious educators
- Activity books have thin margins, require volume
- Competition from free teacher resource sites

## Unique Angle: The Educational Logic Puzzle Format

Unlike standard logic grid puzzles with fictional scenarios:
- Each puzzle encodes REAL FACTS students need to learn
- Solving the puzzle = learning and retaining the content
- Curriculum-aligned (state standards, AP content, etc.)
- Can cover: history sequences, scientific classifications, literary analysis, geography, government structure

**Example - "Kings of England" Puzzle**:
Categories: King | Reign Start | Queen Consort | Key Event
- Students use clues to deduce: Henry VIII / 1509 / Anne Boleyn / English Reformation
- By solving, they learn the associations they'd otherwise memorize
