**Date**: 2026-01-07
**Status**: [Feasibility Analysis]

## Executive Summary

**Overall Feasibility**: Medium

**Key Finding**: Technically straightforward with existing puzzle generation capability, but content creation is the bottleneck. Financial viability depends on achieving efficient puzzle production (~2-4 hours per quality puzzle) and finding the right distribution mix between TPT (easy, lower margin) and activity books (harder, better margin).

**Major Obstacles**:
- Content creation time: Quality curriculum-aligned puzzles require research + puzzle design
- Discovery/marketing: Standing out on TPT requires consistent effort
- Activity book route: Requires upfront investment and publisher relationships or self-pub expertise

**Investment Required**:
- Time: 100-200 hours to launch initial product line
- Money: $500-2,000 startup + $50-100/month ongoing (minimal infrastructure)

**Timeline Estimate**: 2-3 months to first products on TPT; 6-9 months for activity book

## Technical Feasibility

**Complexity Rating**: 2/5 (Low-Medium)

**Justification**: Core puzzle mechanics already exist in separate puzzle app project. Main work is content creation (curriculum research) rather than technical development. No complex infrastructure needed.

**Technology Requirements**:
- **Puzzle generation**: Existing capability from logic-puzzle-app project can be adapted
- **Content delivery**: PDF generation for TPT, print-ready files for activity books
- **Design software**: Canva/InDesign for professional formatting (~$13/month for Canva Pro or one-time InDesign)
- **Distribution platform**: TPT account ($29 one-time Basic or $60/year Premium)

**Development Approach**:
- Self-build leveraging existing puzzle generation capability
- Template-based production for efficiency
- Rationale: Low technical complexity, mostly content work

**Timeline Estimate**:
- MVP (first puzzle pack on TPT): 4-6 weeks
- Full subject series (10-15 packs): 3-4 months
- Activity book (print-ready): 6-9 months
- Buffer added: 25% for unknowns

**Technical Risks**:
- **Puzzle quality consistency**: Risk of creating puzzles that are too easy, too hard, or have multiple solutions
  - Mitigation: Testing framework, beta testers (teachers)
- **Curriculum accuracy**: Getting facts wrong would be embarrassing/damaging
  - Mitigation: Research verification process, fact-checking, teacher review

**Score**: 4/5

## Operational Feasibility

**Content Creation Process**:
1. Select curriculum topic (e.g., "American Revolution - Key Figures")
2. Research facts (primary sources, textbooks, standards)
3. Design puzzle grid (categories, relationships)
4. Write clues that encode facts logically
5. Test solve to ensure single solution
6. Format for print/digital delivery
7. Create answer key with educational explanations

**Time Estimates Per Puzzle**:
| Task | Time (hours) |
|------|--------------|
| Curriculum research | 0.5-1 |
| Puzzle design | 1-2 |
| Clue writing | 0.5-1 |
| Testing/verification | 0.25-0.5 |
| Formatting | 0.25-0.5 |
| **Total per puzzle** | **2.5-5 hours** |

**Daily/Weekly/Monthly Requirements**:
- **Daily**: None required (not a service business)
- **Weekly**: 5-10 hours content creation during active development; 1-2 hours maintenance once established
- **Monthly**: TPT store updates, marketing posts, customer feedback review

**Operational Complexity**: Low

**Solo-Viable?**: Yes - This is a content business, not a service business. No customer support requirements, no real-time operations.

**Key Operational Tasks**:
- Content creation (core activity)
- TPT store management (listing, SEO, updates)
- Marketing (Pinterest, teacher blogs, social)
- Activity book production (if pursuing)
- Customer feedback monitoring

**Regulatory Requirements**:
- None for digital downloads
- ISBN registration for books (optional but recommended)
- Standard self-employment tax considerations

**Operational Risks**:
- **Burnout**: Content creation can be tedious at scale
- **Quality drift**: Pressure to produce more may reduce quality
- **Subject expertise**: Some subjects require more research than others

**Score**: 4/5

## Market Timing

**Timing Assessment**: Right Time

**Tailwinds** (favorable trends):
- **Post-COVID engagement crisis**: Teachers actively seeking new engagement tools
- **Homeschool growth**: Market expanded 7%+ annually, sticky post-pandemic
- **Critical thinking emphasis**: Standards increasingly prioritize reasoning
- **Puzzle book renaissance**: Murdle proved demand exists (600K copies sold)
- **Print-at-home acceptance**: Teachers comfortable with digital → print workflow

**Headwinds** (unfavorable trends):
- **AI tutoring emergence**: ChatGPT, Khanmigo may reduce worksheet demand long-term
- **Teacher budget pressure**: Economic uncertainty affects discretionary spending
- **Free resource proliferation**: More free alternatives appearing
- **Platform competition**: TPT facing competition from other marketplaces

**Market Readiness**: Yes - Teachers already buy logic puzzles, just not curriculum-aligned ones. Behavior change is minimal.

## Go/No-Go Indicators

**Proceed if**:
- First puzzle pack sells 10+ copies in 60 days
- Positive teacher feedback on curriculum alignment value
- Content creation time stabilizes at <4 hours/puzzle

**Reconsider if**:
- First puzzle pack sells <5 copies in 60 days
- Feedback indicates puzzles too hard/easy or curriculum alignment not valued
- Content creation takes >6 hours/puzzle consistently

**Stop if**:
- No sales after 90 days despite quality products
- Content creation proves unsustainably time-intensive
- Market research reveals fatal flaw not previously identified
