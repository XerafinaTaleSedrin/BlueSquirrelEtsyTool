# Decision Framework: Educational Logic Puzzles for Educators

**Date**: 2026-01-07
**Research Time Invested**: ~4 hours
**Decision**: GO (Conditional - with validation experiment)
**Confidence**: Medium

---

## Executive Summary

Educational logic puzzles that teach curriculum content (history, science, etc.) represent a viable niche opportunity within the validated educational resources market. The core insight is that logic puzzles can encode real facts students need to learn, creating "learning through deduction" rather than rote memorization.

Market research confirms demand exists (TPT sellers earn from logic puzzles, Mind Benders is a bestseller, Murdle proved puzzle book appetite). Technical feasibility is high given existing puzzle generation capability. Financial viability is modest but achievable with disciplined content creation.

**Recommendation**: Proceed with a low-risk validation experiment. Create one test puzzle pack, list on Teachers Pay Teachers, and measure demand before scaling.

## SWOT Analysis

**Strengths** (Internal advantages):
- Existing puzzle generation capability reduces technical lift
- Low startup cost minimizes financial risk
- Curriculum alignment is a clear differentiator from generic puzzles
- Solo-viable operation with no service overhead
- Multiple revenue channels (TPT, activity books, museum gift shops)

**Weaknesses** (Internal disadvantages):
- Content creation is labor-intensive (2-5 hours per puzzle)
- Limited curriculum expertise requires research per subject
- No existing audience or TPT store credibility
- Competing with free resources and teacher expectations of cheap
- Activity book production requires new skills

**Opportunities** (External positive factors):
- No dominant player in "curriculum-aligned logic puzzles" niche
- Homeschool market growing 7%+ with higher willingness to pay
- Murdle proved puzzle books can be hits - same format, different content
- Museum gift shops underserved with themed educational products
- Teacher engagement crisis creates demand for novel materials
- Critical thinking emphasis in standards creates curriculum justification

**Threats** (External negative factors):
- TPT has 85% of sellers earning <$500/year - power law distribution
- AI tutoring tools (ChatGPT, Khanmigo) may reduce worksheet demand
- Free alternatives proliferating (Woo! Jr., etc.)
- Teacher budget constraints limit discretionary spending
- Platform dependency on TPT (20% fee, algorithm changes)
- Activity book margins thin without significant volume

---

## Critical Assumptions

Assumptions that must hold true for success:

1. **Teachers value curriculum alignment enough to pay premium**
   - Impact if wrong: Products don't sell; compete on price with generic puzzles (race to bottom)
   - How to validate: Test pack with explicit curriculum alignment messaging vs. generic positioning

2. **Content creation can be efficient (2-4 hours per quality puzzle)**
   - Impact if wrong: Unit economics don't work; can't produce enough content profitably
   - How to validate: Track time on first 10 puzzles, refine workflow

3. **TPT discovery is achievable with quality + SEO**
   - Impact if wrong: Products languish undiscovered despite quality
   - How to validate: Monitor views, click-through, conversion on first products

4. **Puzzles work pedagogically (students actually learn from solving)**
   - Impact if wrong: Value proposition collapses; just another worksheet
   - How to validate: Teacher feedback, ideally classroom testing

---

## Key Uncertainties

What we still don't know:

- **Optimal price point**
  - Why it matters: Too high = no sales; too low = not worth effort
  - To resolve: A/B test $3.99 vs. $4.99 vs. $5.99
  - Cost to validate: 2-3 product variations, 60-90 days observation

- **Which subjects have most demand**
  - Why it matters: History ≠ Science ≠ Literature in demand
  - To resolve: Create one pack per subject, compare sales
  - Cost to validate: 30-40 hours content creation

- **Activity book viability**
  - Why it matters: Better margins but higher risk than TPT
  - To resolve: After TPT validation, test with KDP book
  - Cost to validate: $200-400 production, 6+ months timeline

- **Repeat purchase behavior**
  - Why it matters: LTV depends on teachers buying multiple packs
  - To resolve: Track repeat customers after 6 months
  - Cost to validate: Time only

## Decision Reasoning

This idea passes the minimum thresholds for a "proceed with validation" decision:
- No dimension below 2 (lowest is Financial at 3/5)
- Average score above 3.0 (3.6/5)
- Multiple dimensions at 4/5 (Technical, Operational)

The opportunity is **niche but real**. Educational logic puzzles exist and sell. The gap is systematic curriculum alignment - no one dominates "logic puzzles that teach US History standards" or similar. This is a differentiation opportunity.

The **risk profile is favorable**: Low startup cost ($500-700), solo-viable, leverages existing capability. Downside is limited to time invested. Upside is modest but achievable ($1,000-10,000/year) with potential for activity book expansion if digital validates.

The **key constraint is content creation efficiency**. If puzzles take 5+ hours each, unit economics are marginal. If they can be produced in 2-3 hours, the business model works.

**Key Factors That Drove This Decision**:
- Market validated: Teachers buy logic puzzles, homeschool market growing
- Technical leverage: Existing puzzle generation capability
- Low risk: Minimal investment, easy to stop if it doesn't work
- Clear differentiation: Curriculum alignment is underserved

**Decision Criteria Applied**:
- Minimum thresholds: All met (no dimension <2, average >3.0, has strengths at 4)
- Deal-breakers: None identified
- Must-haves: Existing capability leverage, solo-viable, low startup cost - all present

**Tradeoff Analysis**:
- What we're trading off: Time (100-200 hours for initial validation)
- Opportunity cost: Could spend time on other projects (logic-puzzle-app consumer version, other ideas)
- Risk tolerance: Low financial risk, moderate time risk, acceptable given learning value

## Next Steps

### Phase 1: Validation Experiment (Weeks 1-8)

**Immediate Actions** (Week 1-2):
1. Set up TPT Premium seller account ($60)
2. Research US History - Revolutionary War curriculum standards (state standards, AP requirements)
3. Design first puzzle pack: 5 puzzles covering key Revolutionary War figures/events
4. Create professional formatting template in Canva

**Content Creation** (Week 2-4):
1. Create 5 Revolutionary War logic puzzles (target: 15-20 hours total)
2. Test-solve all puzzles for single solution
3. Fact-check all curriculum content
4. Create answer key with educational explanations
5. Design cover and preview images

**Launch & Monitor** (Week 4-8):
1. List on TPT with optimized title, description, keywords
2. Create free sample puzzle as lead magnet
3. Post in 2-3 teacher Facebook groups for initial visibility
4. Track: Views, favorites, sales, reviews
5. Collect any teacher feedback

**Validation Success Criteria** (After 60 days):
| Metric | Proceed | Reconsider | Stop |
|--------|---------|------------|------|
| Sales | 10+ | 5-9 | <5 |
| Views | 500+ | 200-499 | <200 |
| Reviews | 2+ positive | 1 positive | Negative |
| Time/puzzle | <4 hours | 4-6 hours | >6 hours |

### Phase 2: If Validation Succeeds (Months 3-6)

1. Create 2-3 more subject packs (different subjects to test demand)
2. Build bundle products for higher AOV
3. Establish content creation rhythm (2-3 puzzles/week)
4. Begin audience building (Pinterest, teacher blog outreach)
5. Evaluate activity book opportunity based on TPT data

### Phase 3: Scale/Expand (Months 6-12)

1. Comprehensive subject coverage for top-selling subjects
2. Launch first activity book via KDP (compile best puzzles)
3. Explore museum gift shop distribution for history themes
4. Consider licensing discussions if significant traction

## Lessons Learned (For Playbook)

**Market Insights**:
- Educational content markets are fragmented with low barriers to entry
- Teachers Pay Teachers is proven distribution but highly competitive (4M+ products)
- "Curriculum alignment" is underutilized as differentiation in supplemental materials
- Murdle success (600K copies) proves puzzle books can break out with right positioning

**Research Process**:
- Web search for market sizing data works well for educational markets
- TPT itself is excellent primary research source (search, browse top sellers, pricing)
- Activity book/publishing data is harder to find than SaaS/tech markets

**Pattern Recognition**:
- Low barrier + fragmented market = need clear differentiation
- Content businesses require sustained effort before payoff
- Validation experiments reduce risk but require discipline to actually run

## Related Ideas (If This Doesn't Work)

- **Pivot A**: Focus on homeschool market exclusively (higher willingness to pay, less price sensitive)
