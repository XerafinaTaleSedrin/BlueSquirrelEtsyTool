# Idea Backlog - Quick Capture

**Purpose**: Rapid capture of random ideas without commitment to research.

---

## How to Use This Backlog

This is your **idea inbox** - a low-friction place to capture random ideas as they come to you.

### Capture Freely
- Write down any idea that crosses your mind
- No judgment, no filtering
- One-line descriptions are fine
- Don't worry about duplicates or quality

### Research Selectively
- Review this list periodically (weekly or monthly)
- Pick the most promising ideas to research
- Archive or delete ideas that lose appeal
- Use: `"Research idea: [NAME]"` to formally research an idea

### Keep It Clean
- Delete ideas you're no longer interested in
- Move researched ideas out of this list (they go to portfolio)
- Archive ideas that are "maybe someday but not now"
- Keep this list under 20-30 items (if it grows too large, prune)

## Notes

**Where ideas come from**:
- Personal frustrations or problems you experience
- Conversations with friends/colleagues
- News articles or tech trends
- Customer complaints you observe
- Gaps in existing markets
- "I wish there was..." moments
- Random shower thoughts
- Industry changes or regulatory shifts

**Tips for idea capture**:
- Write them down immediately (don't trust memory)
- Include just enough detail to remember later
- Note the source (where did this idea come from?)
- Tag with urgency if time-sensitive
- Don't self-censor during capture

**When to research an idea**:
- You keep thinking about it
- You've seen the problem multiple times
- There's some urgency or timing factor
- You have unique insight or advantage
- You're genuinely curious

**When to delete an idea**:
- Lost interest over time
- Realized it's not actually a problem
- Found out it exists and is well-served
- Outside your capability or interests
- Just not exciting anymore
