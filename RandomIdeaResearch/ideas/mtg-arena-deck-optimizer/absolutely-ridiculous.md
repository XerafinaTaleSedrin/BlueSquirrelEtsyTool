# Absolutely Ridiculous — The Dream Factory

**Format**: Historic
**Archetype**: Selesnya Exponential Landfall Insanity
**Strategy**: Ramp to 6 lands, then every land you play creates an EXPONENTIALLY growing army of 4/4 flying indestructible angels that each make everything bigger.

**Ridiculousness Rating**: 11/10

---

## The Ridiculous Math

Here's what happens when the engine is running:

**Board state**: 3x Scute Swarm + Anointed Procession + Divine Visitation + Cathars' Crusade

You play a land:
- 3 Scute Swarms each trigger (3 copies)
- Anointed Procession doubles them (6 copies)
- Divine Visitation: they're all 4/4 flying Angels, not bugs
- Cathars' Crusade: 6 creatures enter = +6/+6 to EVERYTHING
- You now have 9 Scute Swarms (they copied before becoming angels)

Next land:
- 9 triggers, doubled = 18 angels
- Cathars' Crusade: +18/+18 to everything
- Soul Warden: gain 18 life

Next land:
- 27 triggers, doubled = 54 angels, all get +54/+54

**With Traverse the Outlands** (gets lands equal to your biggest creature's power):
- Your biggest creature is a 30/30? Get 30 lands.
- Each land triggers the above exponential chain.
- Final board: approximately A LOT of angels, each approximately A LOT/A LOT big.
- Also you have approximately A LOT life from Soul Warden.
- Then Aetherflux Reservoir: pay 50 life, deal 50 damage. You have 4000 life. Do it 80 times.

## The Dream Game (How It Actually Plays Out)

### The Setup (Turns 1-4)
```
Turn 1: Soul Warden. Innocent. Harmless. They don't know what's coming.
Turn 2: Lotus Cobra. "Oh ramp, how cute" — opponent, probably.
Turn 3: Cultivate. Get 2 lands. Cobra makes mana. Play Scute Swarm with the bonus mana.
Turn 4: Cultivate again. 6 lands now. Play a land from hand.
        Scute Swarm triggers: copy itself.
        Cobra makes mana. Soul Warden gains life.
        Opponent: "Hmm."
```

### The Escalation (Turns 5-6)
```
Turn 5: Anointed Procession. Play land.
        2 Scute Swarms trigger, doubled = 4 new Scutes.
        Now you have 7 Scute Swarms.
        Opponent: "Oh no."

Turn 6: Divine Visitation. Play land.
        7 Scutes trigger, doubled = 14 angels (4/4 flying).
        Soul Warden gains 14 life.
        Opponent: "OH NO."
```

### The Absurdity (Turn 7)
```
Turn 7: Traverse the Outlands. Your biggest Scute is a 1/1...
        wait, Cathars' Crusade was out? Everything got +14/+14 last turn?
        Traverse gets 15 basic lands.

        Land 1: 7 Scutes trigger (doubled = 14 angels, +14/+14 to all)
        Land 2: 21 Scutes trigger (doubled = 42 angels, +42/+42 to all)
        Land 3: 63 Scutes trigger (doubled = 126 angels, +126/+126 to all)

        ...Arena runs out of memory and crashes.
        You win by default. Probably.

        Total creatures on board: ≈10,000+
        Total life gained from Soul Warden: ≈10,000+
        Aetherflux Reservoir activations available: ≈200

        Opponent: has left the match.
```

## Why This Is Ridiculous

1. **Exponential growth** — Scute Swarm doesn't add, it MULTIPLIES. Each copy copies itself next landfall
2. **Every piece amplifies every other piece** — tokens trigger lifegain trigger Cathars' Crusade triggers bigger Traverse triggers more lands triggers more Scutes
3. **Multiple "I win" buttons** — Exponential angels, Aetherflux laser, Approach of the Second Sun, or just attacking with 10,000 creatures
4. **Avacyn makes it permanent** — Can't board wipe. Can't remove anything. It's all indestructible. They have to exile SPECIFICALLY Avacyn
5. **Vorinclex is just mean** — Opponent's lands don't untap. They can't do ANYTHING while you're making thousands of angels
6. **The game client genuinely might crash** — Arena has a token limit and this deck exceeds it regularly

---

## Weaknesses (Being Honest)

- **Slow to start** — needs 4-5 turns of setup. Aggro decks might kill you first
- **Vulnerable before the engine** — if they kill Scute Swarm early, you're just ramping for nothing
- **Arena has a 250-token limit** — you'll hit it and stop making tokens. Luckily 250 angels is usually enough
- **Your opponent might report you** — not a real weakness but they'll be mad
