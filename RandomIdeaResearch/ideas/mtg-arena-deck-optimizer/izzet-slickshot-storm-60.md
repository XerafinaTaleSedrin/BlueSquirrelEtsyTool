# Izzet Slickshot Storm (Historic Bo1)

**Format:** Historic | **Cards:** 60 | **Colors:** UR
**Archetype:** Prowess/Storm Hybrid
**Status:** Built from confirmed collection (untapped.gg export 2026-03-14)

## Why This Deck

Slickshot Show-Off is the most broken creature in Historic. Double strike + +2/+0 per noncreature spell means plotting it on turn 2 and chaining 4 spells on turn 3 = 20+ damage in one combat. Turn 3 kills are routine.

**The novel twist:** This build runs Grapeshot as a storm backup finisher. Mythic opponents will board in creature removal for Slickshot — but you can chain spells and Grapeshot them for 8-12 regardless. They literally cannot sideboard correctly against you.

**Creature plan:** Slickshot double-strike kills, Swiftspear prowess beatdown, Sprite Dragon growing into a 6/6+ flyer.
**Storm plan:** Chain 6+ cheap spells, Grapeshot for lethal (storm count 6 = 7 damage, usually finishing after early burn/combat damage).

## The Turn 3 Kill

```
T1: Mountain, Kumano Faces Kakkazan
    (Chapter I: your spells deal +1 damage this turn — irrelevant now)

T2: Land, PLOT Slickshot Show-Off (pay 1R, exile it face-up)
    (Kumano Chapter II: +1/+1 counter to a creature you control)

T3: Cast Slickshot from plot FOR FREE (2/2 double strike)
    Kumano Chapter III: becomes 2/2 creature with haste

    Now chain:
    → Crash Through (R): Slickshot +2/+0 = 4/2, gains trample, draw a card
    → Opt (U): Slickshot +2/+0 = 6/2, dig for more fuel
    → Play with Fire (R): 2 damage to face, Slickshot +2/+0 = 8/2
    → Shock (R): 2 damage to face, Slickshot +2/+0 = 10/2

    Attack: 10/2 double strike with trample = 20 combat damage
    + 4 burn damage = 24 TOTAL (opponent started at 20, very dead)
```

Even casting just 3 spells: 8/2 double strike = 16 combat damage. Add any burn and that's lethal.

## Gameplay Guide

### Mulligan
**Snap keep:** Land (R source) + Slickshot or Swiftspear + 2+ cheap spells
**Great:** Kumano + Slickshot + land + any cantrips
**Good:** 2 lands + Sprite Dragon + cantrips (slower but Sprite grows fast)
**Keepable:** 1 land + multiple 1-mana spells (risky but this deck runs on 2 lands)
**Mulligan:** No creatures, no red source, 4+ lands

### Sequencing
**With Slickshot in hand:**
- T1: Kumano or Swiftspear
- T2: Plot Slickshot (don't cast it — plotting is the key)
- T3: Cast Slickshot for free, then chain ALL your cheap spells. Crash Through first (trample), then burn + cantrips. Attack for 16-24.

**Without Slickshot:**
- T1: Swiftspear, attack
- T2: Sprite Dragon or Kumano + burn
- T3+: Chain spells, grow Sprite Dragon, burn face. Grapeshot when storm count is 5+.

### When to Go Face vs Remove Creatures
- **Go face** when you have Slickshot and a hand full of spells. You're faster than almost anything.
- **Remove creatures** only when they have lethal or a blocker that stops your Slickshot kill (like a 4+ toughness creature with your Slickshot at 6 power double strike — you'd still push through with trample from Crash Through).
- Play with Fire's scry helps find the last points of damage.

### The Grapeshot Plan
When they remove your creatures (or you're facing a board stall):
1. Chain cantrips: Opt, Consider, Crash Through (draw into more spells)
2. Fire off burn: Play with Fire, Shock to face
3. Grapeshot: storm count = number of spells cast this turn. Each copy targets their face.
4. Example: Opt → Consider → Crash Through → Shock → Play with Fire → Grapeshot
   = storm count 6, Grapeshot makes 6 copies = 7 damage + the 4 from earlier burn = 11 total

### Against Specific Matchups

**vs Aggro (Mono-Red mirror, White Weenie):**
- You're faster. Plot Slickshot, kill them turn 3-4.
- Use burn on their creatures only if they threaten lethal before you can combo off.

**vs Midrange (Rakdos, Jund):**
- They'll Thoughtseize your Slickshot. That's fine — you have 4 copies.
- Sprite Dragon is great here — they often can't remove a growing flyer efficiently.
- Grapeshot punishes them for tapping out for Sheoldred.

**vs Control (Azorius, Dimir):**
- Deploy threats under their countermagic (1-2 mana creatures).
- Bait their counters with burn, then resolve Slickshot.
- Grapeshot is UNCOUNTERABLE in effect — they'd need to counter each storm copy individually. Storm copies aren't "cast" so they can't counter the copies at all.

**vs Combo:**
- You're one of the fastest decks in the format. Race them.
- Turn 3 kill beats almost every combo deck.
