# Selesnya Squirrel Vanguard — Focused 62-Card Combo

**Format**: Historic
**Rank Target**: Diamond
**Archetype**: Green-White Lifegain Combo
**Strategy**: Gain life, put +1/+1 counters on Scurry Oak, generate squirrel tokens that all enter as copies of Thunderbond Vanguard. Three pieces on board = infinite creatures that are each X/X where X = your creature count. Conclave Mentor and token doublers accelerate the kill. Trelasarra scries you to the combo while growing into a backup threat.

**Trimmed from**: 80-card Yorion build. Cut all 5+ drops (Vorinclex, Elesh Norn, Craterhoof, Defiler, Tendershoot Dryad), cut Worldweave/Tribute/Growing Rites/Great Henge/Halo Fountain, dropped Yorion companion. Every remaining card directly feeds the Scurry Oak + Thunderbond Vanguard loop.

---

## The Combo

```
CORE LOOP (3 pieces = infinite):

  Soul Warden + Scurry Oak + Heliod, Sun-Crowned

  1. Any creature enters → Soul Warden gains 1 life
  2. Gain life → Heliod puts +1/+1 counter on Scurry Oak
  3. Counter on Scurry Oak → creates 1/1 Squirrel token
  4. Squirrel enters → Soul Warden gains 1 life
  5. → Step 2. Repeat forever.

THE VANGUARD TWIST:

  Thunderbond Vanguard on the battlefield:
  → Every token enters as a COPY of Thunderbond Vanguard
  → Each copy has power/toughness = number of creatures you control
  → 250 tokens = 250 copies of a 250/250
  → Attack. Very dead.

ACCELERATORS:

  Conclave Mentor on board:
  → Heliod puts 1 counter → Mentor adds 1 more → 2 counters
  → 2 counters = 2 squirrels = 2 life = 4 counters next cycle
  → Goes exponential even without full infinite assembly

  Anointed Procession / Parallel Lives:
  → Each squirrel becomes 2 squirrels (or 4 with both)
  → 4 squirrels = 4 life = 4+ counters = 16 squirrels
  → Hits Arena's 250 token cap in 2 cycles

BACKUP COMBO (no Heliod needed):

  Conclave Mentor + Scurry Oak + Soul Warden/Ajani's Welcome
  → Cast any creature → Soul Warden gains life
  → Trelasarra gets +1/+1 counter (she triggers on lifegain)
  → If you have ANY way to put a counter on Scurry Oak, the chain starts
  → Conclave Mentor doubles the counter → 2 squirrels → 2 life triggers
  → Not truly infinite without Heliod, but generates massive boards fast
```

## How It Plays

### Turn 1: Lifegain Online
- **Soul Warden** — starts gaining life from every creature (yours and theirs)
- **Ajani's Welcome** — same as Soul Warden but survives creature wipes
- **Commune with Beavers** — dig for a combo piece if your hand needs it
- **Alseid** — lifelink body + protection ready for later

### Turn 2: Grow and Filter
- **Trelasarra** — every lifegain trigger = +1/+1 AND scry 1. She grows while you filter to combo pieces
- **Conclave Mentor** — now every counter is doubled. Sets up explosive Scurry Oak turns
- With Soul Warden already out, any creature entering starts growing Trelasarra immediately

### Turn 3: Deploy Combo Pieces
- **Scurry Oak** — now every +1/+1 counter makes a squirrel. With Soul Warden + Conclave Mentor, each creature entering can cascade into multiple squirrels
- **Thunderbond Vanguard** — now every token enters as a copy. Even without going infinite, a few squirrels becoming 5/5+ copies is a massive board
- **Heliod** — if you have Soul Warden + Scurry Oak, dropping Heliod = infinite right now

### Turn 4: Win
- **Full combo**: Soul Warden + Scurry Oak + Heliod = infinite squirrels. Thunderbond on board = infinite copies of a 250/250. Attack with everything
- **No Heliod yet?** Conclave Mentor + Scurry Oak + Soul Warden still generates a huge board. Thunderbond makes every token a serious threat
- **Token doubler**: Anointed Procession or Parallel Lives turns a moderate combo turn into an overwhelming one

### The Kill Turn
```
Board: Soul Warden + Scurry Oak + Heliod + Thunderbond Vanguard

Any creature enters or any lifegain trigger:
→ Heliod puts +1/+1 counter on Scurry Oak
→ Scurry Oak creates a Squirrel token
→ Token enters as a COPY of Thunderbond Vanguard
→ Soul Warden gains 1 life (creature entered)
→ Heliod puts another counter on Scurry Oak
→ Another Squirrel → another Thunderbond copy → INFINITE

Result: 250 tokens, each is a copy of Thunderbond Vanguard
Each copy's P/T = 250+
Attack for 62,500+ damage.
```

## Diamond Gameplan

### What You're Trying to Do Every Game
1. Stick a lifegain piece (Soul Warden or Ajani's Welcome) early
2. Deploy Scurry Oak + Thunderbond Vanguard on turn 3
3. Find Heliod to close the loop (Commune digs, Trelasarra scries)
4. Go infinite, attack with Thunderbond army

### Against Aggro (Mono-Red, White Weenie)
- Soul Warden is your MVP — gaining life off THEIR creatures buys time
- Trelasarra grows fast when they play lots of small creatures
- Path to Exile their biggest threat
- You can often race: Thunderbond + a few tokens = massive blockers
- Blossoming Sands lifegain matters more than you'd think

### Against Control (Azorius, Dimir)
- Deploy threats before they have mana for counters (1-2 CMC creatures)
- Ajani's Welcome is better than Soul Warden here (survives board wipes)
- Hold Heroic Intervention for their wrath
- Hold Blossoming Defense / Alseid for targeted removal on combo pieces
- Heliod is indestructible — they can't destroy him, only exile
- You only need ONE turn with the full combo. Be patient, assemble, then fire

### Against Midrange (Rakdos, Jund, Gruul)
- Path their early threats to buy time
- Conclave Mentor + Trelasarra can build a board that trades well
- The combo kills through any board state — you don't need to outgrind them
- Watch for Thoughtseize taking Heliod — Commune to find a replacement

### Against Combo (Other combo decks)
- You're faster than most combos. Turn 3-4 kill is realistic
- Path to Exile disrupts creature-based combos
- Race them. Don't try to interact, just assemble and fire

## Mulligan Guide

**Snap keep**: Land + Soul Warden/Ajani's Welcome + Scurry Oak or Thunderbond
**Great**: 2-3 lands (G and W) + lifegain piece + any combo creature
**Good**: Land + Commune with Beavers + any 2-drop (dig turn 1, deploy turn 2)
**Keepable**: Alseid/Blossoming Defense + combo piece + lands (protection-first hand)
**Mulligan**: No green source, no lifegain creature, all 4-drops, 5+ lands, no creatures

**Key principle**: You need a lifegain enabler (Soul Warden or Ajani's Welcome) in your opening hand or a way to find one (Commune). Without lifegain, the combo doesn't fire.

## Protection Cheat Sheet

| Their Play | Your Answer |
|------------|-------------|
| Kill spell on Scurry Oak / Heliod | Alseid (sac, W: protection from a color) |
| Kill spell on any combo piece | Blossoming Defense (G: hexproof +2/+2) |
| Board wipe (Wrath, Farewell) | Heroic Intervention (1G: hexproof + indestructible) |
| Big creature threatening lethal | Path to Exile (W: exile it) |
| Heliod targeted by exile | Can't save with protection — play around it, hold a 2nd if possible |
| Multiple removal spells in one turn | Deploy threats one at a time. Don't overcommit until you can combo in one shot |

**Rule of thumb**: If you have the full combo in hand, don't slam everything into removal. Deploy the lifegain piece first (lowest value target). Next turn add Scurry Oak (they might not recognize the threat). Thunderbond next. Heliod last — he completes the loop and they get zero priority to respond before the first trigger.

## Why This Build for Diamond

1. **Focused combo** — every card feeds the Scurry Oak + Thunderbond loop. No dilution from 5-6 drops that sit in hand
2. **Fast clock** — average CMC 1.92 means you're deploying 2 spells per turn while they play 1
3. **Redundancy** — 8 lifegain enablers (4 Soul Warden + 4 Ajani's Welcome), 4 Commune to dig, Trelasarra scries every trigger
4. **Wins through interaction** — Heliod is indestructible. Ajani's Welcome survives creature wipes. Heroic Intervention blanks wraths. You only need ONE turn with the combo assembled
5. **Thunderbond changes the math** — even without going fully infinite, 3-4 squirrels becoming 7/7 copies of Vanguard is lethal. You don't always need the full combo to win
6. **Conclave Mentor as insurance** — doubles counters so even a partial combo (no Heliod) generates massive boards. When Mentor dies, distributes its counters as a parting gift
7. **22 lands is correct** — 17 one-drops means you always have turn-1 plays. Fabled Passage thins the deck. Blossoming Sands triggers your lifegain cards for free

## Upgrade Path

If you pick up more copies or wildcards:
- **3rd Heliod** — more consistent combo. Finding Heliod is the bottleneck; a 3rd copy fixes that
- **2nd Anointed Procession** — doubling tokens is the difference between "big board" and "instant kill"
- **2nd Heroic Intervention** — Diamond players pack more wraths. Second copy = more insurance
- **Collected Company** (3G) — instant speed, top 6, put 2 creatures onto battlefield. Hits every creature in the deck. End-step CoCo into Scurry Oak + Soul Warden is devastating
- **Temple Garden** — untapped GW dual replacing a Blossoming Sands for speed
