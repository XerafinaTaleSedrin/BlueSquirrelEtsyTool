# Mono-Red Aggro (Standard Bo1)

**Format:** Standard | **Cards:** 60 | **Colors:** R
**Archetype:** Red Deck Wins / Prowess Aggro
**Status:** Built from confirmed collection (untapped.gg export 2026-03-14)

## Strategy

Kill them before they do anything. 20 one-drops, every spell is burn or pump. Slickshot Show-Off does the same thing here as in Historic — plot turn 2, chain spells turn 3, deal 20+.

The Standard version trades the blue cantrips (Opt/Consider) for more burn and Heartfire Hero's valiant triggers. Less card selection, more raw aggression.

## Why These Choices

**Phoenix Chick over Cacophony Scamp:** Flying evasion matters more in Standard (lots of ground blockers). Plus it recurs from the graveyard, giving you reach in grindy games.

**2 Monstrous Rage (not 4):** Only have 2. Would run 4 if available — it's the best pump spell in Standard (trample + permanent +1/+1 from Monster Role). Fill the gap with more burn.

**Urabrask's Forge:** This card wins games control decks think they've stabilized. They wrath your board, Forge keeps making bigger tokens every turn. They can't remove it efficiently.

**No Torch the Tower:** Don't own it. Shock + Burst Lightning fill the same role. Torch would be an upgrade (1 damage + bargain for 3 + exile is premium removal).

## Gameplay Notes

**Mulligan:** Keep any hand with a 1-drop creature + land + burn/pump. Mulliganing aggressively to find a turn 1 creature is correct — this deck can't win without early pressure.

**Sequencing:**
- T1: Heartfire Hero or Swiftspear (always lead with a creature)
- T2: Plot Slickshot OR play another 1-drop + Shock/burn
- T3: Cast plotted Slickshot + chain spells for lethal, OR Emberheart Challenger/Forge
- T4+: If they're still alive, use burn to close + Phoenix Chick recursion

**Heartfire Hero targeting:** Every time you target Heartfire Hero with a spell (Monstrous Rage, even Shock on your own Hero if desperate), it gets a +1/+1 counter. When it dies, it deals damage equal to its power. So a Hero that's been pumped twice is a 4/4 that deals 4 when it dies.

**Urabrask's Forge timing:** Play on turn 3 if the board is stable. Don't play it when you're behind — you need the mana for burn to survive.

## Potential Upgrades

- **+2 Monstrous Rage** (Uncommon) — the #1 upgrade, best pump spell in Standard
- **4 Torch the Tower** (Common) — replace Shock, better in every way with bargain
- **Goddric, Cloaked Reveler** (Rare) — 3-mana 3/3 haste, celebration trigger makes him a 4/4 flying dragon
- **Charming Scoundrel** (Rare) — flexible 2-drop, creates treasure/draws/makes 1/1
- **+2 Screaming Nemesis** (Mythic) — incredible against midrange, punishes interaction
