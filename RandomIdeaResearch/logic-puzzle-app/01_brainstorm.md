# Logic Puzzle App - Initial Brainstorm

**Date Created**: 2026-01-04
**Status**: [Brainstorm]
**Priority**: [Medium]

---

## What's the Idea?

A mobile/web app that sells themed narrative logic puzzle packs. Each pack contains 10-15 logic puzzles woven together with a narrative theme (e.g., D&D modules, book-based stories, mystery scenarios). Monetized via freemium model: several free packs to attract users, premium themed narrative packs sold for a few dollars each. Leverages existing puzzle generation capability to create content.

---

## What Problem Does It Solve?

**The Problem**:
Logic puzzle enthusiasts want curated, themed puzzle experiences with narrative engagement beyond standalone puzzles. Current puzzle apps are often generic collections without cohesive storytelling or thematic depth.

**Who Experiences This Problem?**:
- Logic puzzle fans who enjoy themed experiences
- People who like narrative-driven games (point-and-click adventure fans, escape room enthusiasts)
- Casual gamers looking for offline, brain-training entertainment
- Fans of specific IPs (D&D, books, etc.) who want puzzle content in those universes

**How Acute Is the Pain?**:
- [ ] Must-have (people actively search for solutions, willing to pay)
- [x] Should-have (people want it but can work around)
- [ ] Nice-to-have (convenience, not critical)

---

## How Do People Solve It Today?

**Current Solutions**:
1. Generic puzzle apps (Sudoku, crossword, logic grid apps) - no narrative cohesion
2. Physical puzzle books themed around topics - requires physical purchase, not portable
3. Escape room apps - often point-and-click adventure, not pure logic puzzles
4. Standalone web puzzle generators - no curation or theming

**What's Inadequate About Current Solutions?**:
- Generic puzzle apps lack narrative engagement and thematic cohesion
- Physical books aren't as portable/convenient as apps
- Escape room apps focus on hidden object/adventure mechanics rather than pure logic
- No combination of: themed narrative + curated puzzle series + digital convenience

## Initial Viability Thoughts

**Why This Might Work**:
- Already have puzzle generation capability (reduces development complexity)
- Proven freemium model in puzzle app market
- Niche differentiation: narrative-themed logic puzzle packs
- Low content creation cost once pipeline established
- Potential for licensing/IP partnerships (D&D, books, etc.)
- Recurring revenue potential (release new packs regularly)

**Why This Might Not Work**:
- Saturated puzzle app market - hard to get discovered
- Creating quality narrative content alongside puzzles is time-intensive
- App development/maintenance costs (iOS, Android, web)
- Distribution challenges (app store fees, marketing costs)
- May need IP licenses for premium themes (D&D, books) - cost/complexity
- Customer willingness to pay for puzzle content (expectations of free puzzle apps)
