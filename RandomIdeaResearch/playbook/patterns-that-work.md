# Patterns That Work

**Purpose**: Document patterns and characteristics of viable business ideas discovered through research.

This file grows over time as you evaluate ideas and notice what makes some more viable than others.

---

## How to Use This Playbook

As you research ideas and make decisions:
- **After Go decisions**: Note what made the idea compelling
- **After successful validations**: Document what worked
- **Periodically**: Review to identify recurring patterns

**Update this file** when you notice patterns emerging across multiple ideas.

## Market Characteristics

### Market Patterns That Indicate Viability

*As you research, add patterns you observe:*

**Example patterns** (replace with your own as you learn):
- Growing markets (>10% annual growth) with clear tailwinds
- Markets with 3-7 competitors (not too empty, not too saturated)
- Clear customer pain points with evidence (Reddit discussions, reviews)
- Customers actively searching for solutions (Google Trends showing growth)
- Willingness to pay demonstrated ($X+/month pricing across competitors)

## Customer Segment Patterns

### Customer Characteristics That Signal Opportunity

*What types of customers make ideas more viable?*

**Example patterns**:
- B2B customers with budget authority and clear ROI
- Niche communities with strong identity and gathering places
- Underserved segments willing to pay premium for focused solutions
- Customers with recurring pain (not one-time problem)

---

## Technology Patterns

### Technical Approaches That Reduce Risk

*What technology choices make ideas more feasible?*

**Example patterns**:
- Leveraging existing APIs/platforms (vs. building from scratch)
- No-code/low-code solutions for non-core features
- Proven tech stacks with good documentation and community
- Avoiding bleeding-edge technology unless it's the core differentiator

---

## Pricing Model Patterns

### Revenue Models That Work

*What pricing approaches lead to viable economics?*

**Example patterns**:
- SaaS with monthly recurring revenue ($50-500/month sweet spot for B2B)
- Freemium with clear upgrade path and <10% conversion
- Transaction fees on high-value transactions (2-5% of $X+)
- Annual plans with 20-30% discount (improves cash flow and retention)

---

## Differentiation Patterns

### Successful Differentiation Angles

*How do viable ideas differentiate from competitors?*

**Example patterns**:
- Vertical specialization (deep expertise in one niche vs. horizontal generalist)
- Simpler UX for specific workflow (vs. feature-bloated competitors)
- Different business model (self-service vs. full-service, or vice versa)
- Better integration with specific platform/ecosystem

---

## Timing Patterns

### Market Timing Indicators

*What signals indicate good timing?*

**Example patterns**:
- New enabling technology just matured (not bleeding edge, not old)
- Regulatory change creating new requirements
- Platform shift (web → mobile → AI) creating greenfield opportunity
- Behavior change accelerated by external event (pandemic → remote work)

## Personal Fit Patterns

### What Makes Ideas More Likely to Succeed

*What personal characteristics correlate with success?*

**Example patterns**:
- Domain expertise or unique insight (worked in the industry)
- Personal experience with the problem (you are the customer)
- Existing network or audience in the space
- Intersection of multiple skills (tech + domain + distribution)

## Validation Patterns

### Signals That Validate Ideas Quickly

*What evidence quickly validates or invalidates ideas?*

**Example patterns**:
- Landing page test with >5% email signup rate (strong interest)
- Customer interviews where people offer to pay before product exists
- Competitor with traction but bad reviews on specific pain point
- Active subreddit or community discussing the problem weekly

## Meta-Patterns

### Patterns About Pattern Recognition

*What have you learned about idea evaluation itself?*

**Example meta-patterns**:
- Ideas that feel "too good to be true" usually are
- When all scores are 3/5, idea is mediocre (need at least one strength)
- "Maybe" ideas linger forever - force yes or no
- Best ideas have obvious "why now?" answer
