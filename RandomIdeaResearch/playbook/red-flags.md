# Red Flags

**Purpose**: Document warning signs and patterns to avoid, discovered through research and decisions.

This file helps you recognize problematic ideas faster and avoid repeating mistakes.

---

## How to Use This Playbook

As you research ideas and make decisions:
- **After No-Go decisions**: Note what made the idea unviable
- **When rejecting ideas**: Document the warning signs
- **Before deep research**: Check if idea has known red flags

**Update this file** when you discover new warning signs or patterns to avoid.

## Market Red Flags

### Warning Signs in Market Research

*What market conditions make ideas unviable?*

**Example red flags** (replace with your own discoveries):
- No evidence of people actively searching for solutions (low/declining Google Trends)
- 10+ direct competitors with no clear differentiation
- Market declining >5% annually with no reversal in sight
- Can't find anyone discussing the problem (no Reddit threads, forum posts, etc.)
- All existing solutions are free (signals low willingness to pay)

## Competition Red Flags

### Competitive Situations to Avoid

*When is competition a deal-breaker?*

**Example patterns**:
- Well-funded incumbent with strong network effects (impossible to displace)
- Giants (Google, Amazon, Microsoft) actively investing in the space
- Recent consolidation (M&A activity indicating winner-take-most dynamic)
- Dozens of failed startups with similar approach (learn why they failed)
- Race to the bottom on pricing (unsustainable margins)

## Customer Red Flags

### Warning Signs About Target Customers

*What customer characteristics make ideas hard to monetize?*

**Example patterns**:
- Can't clearly identify who the customer is
- Customers have no budget authority (need multiple approval layers)
- Very long sales cycles (>6 months) with small deal sizes (<$10K)
- Customer acquisition cost higher than lifetime value
- Customers expect everything free (used to free alternatives)

## Technical Red Flags

### Technology Warning Signs

*What technical characteristics indicate high risk?*

**Example patterns**:
- Core idea depends on unproven or experimental technology
- Requires expertise in multiple specialized domains
- Mission-critical system requiring 99.99% uptime from day one
- Deep dependency on single third-party API or platform
- Regulatory or compliance complexity from day one (HIPAA, SOC2, etc.)

## Financial Red Flags

### Economic Warning Signs

*What financial patterns indicate non-viability?*

**Example patterns**:
- Startup costs >$50K with unclear path to revenue
- Unit economics don't work even at scale
- CAC payback period >12 months
- Requires massive user base before monetization (venture-scale only)
- All competitors losing money or recently shut down

## Timing Red Flags

### Market Timing Warning Signs

*When is timing a deal-breaker?*

**Example patterns**:
- Trend already peaked (Google Trends shows clear decline)
- Technology not mature enough (too many unknowns)
- Market consolidating (already have clear winners)
- Regulatory environment uncertain or hostile
- Customer behavior shift hasn't happened yet (need 5+ year wait)

## Personal Fit Red Flags

### Warning Signs About Alignment

*What personal misalignments doom ideas?*

**Example patterns**:
- Gut feel is "meh" after researching (enthusiasm faded quickly)
- Skill gaps are too large to close in reasonable time
- Conflict with personal values or interests
- Don't understand or care about the customer
- Would need to do work you actively dislike daily

## "Why Hasn't This Been Done?" Red Flags

### Concerning Answers to Critical Question

*What answers to "why not?" should worry you?*

**Dangerous answers**:
- "Economics don't work" (customers won't pay enough to cover costs)
- "It's been tried and failed multiple times" (fundamental flaw exists)
- "Regulatory barriers prevent it" (legal/compliance issues)
- "Technology doesn't exist yet" (waiting on breakthrough)
- "Market is too small" (TAM <$10M)
- "No distribution channel exists" (can't reach customers)

**Safe answers** (opportunities):
- "Technology just became viable" (enabling tech matured recently)
- "Market shift created new need" (pandemic, regulation, behavior change)
- "Niche too small for big players" (viable for solo/small team but not VC-scale)

## Research Process Red Flags

### Warning Signs During Evaluation

*What evaluation patterns indicate weak ideas?*

**Example patterns**:
- Can't find reliable market size data (market may not exist)
- All research leads to competitors (nothing unique about idea)
- Keep finding reasons "why not" vs. "why" (bias confirmation)
- Research takes >3 weeks (analysis paralysis - idea not compelling)
- Constantly changing the idea to avoid problems (original idea wasn't viable)

## Early Warning Signs

### Red Flags to Watch During Execution

*If pursuing an idea, what signals indicate trouble?*

**Example early warnings**:
- Validation experiments fail (<1% conversion when expected 5%+)
- Development taking 3x longer than estimated
- Customer acquisition cost 2x higher than assumed
- Competitor launches similar feature before you
- Can't get anyone excited about the solution

## Meta Red Flags

### Warning Signs About Decision-Making

*What patterns indicate bad decision-making?*

**Example meta-patterns**:
- Rationalizing every concern away ("yes, but...")
- Comparing to outlier success stories (not median outcomes)
- Sunk cost reasoning ("I've spent X hours already")
- Ignoring gut feel because data looks okay
- Can't articulate "why now?" clearly
