### Candidate Bio

Alicia Contreras-Donello is a first-generation Mexican-American and first-generation college graduate running to become the first Latina to represent District 14 in the Maryland House of Delegates.

She is the daughter of Latin American immigrants. Her father worked in California agricultural fields before joining a union in Illinois, while her mother worked in factories and office cleaning. Alicia grew up in Aurora, Illinois, in a neighborhood affected by gang violence and poverty, crediting "family, faith, and community" with her success.

She is a mother and wife living in Montgomery County, Maryland with her family. Her children attend public schools in the county.

### Professional Background

**Education:**
- Dual Bachelor's degrees in Marketing and Spanish
- MBA from Western Illinois University
- Member of Lambda Theta Alpha Latin Sorority, Inc.

**Career:**
- Nearly 20 years as a U.S. Foreign Service Officer with USAID (United States Agency for International Development)
- Served under four presidents (both Republican and Democratic administrations)
- International postings: El Salvador, Tanzania, Afghanistan, Pakistan, Libya, and Washington, D.C.
- Focus areas: Economic development, institutional strengthening, community development
- Previous experience as substitute teacher
- Private sector experience in early career

### Political Background

- First-time candidate for elected office
- No prior elected positions
- Career in non-partisan diplomatic service
