## Applicability to Federal Races

**Important:** Federal races (US House, US Senate) are governed by **FEC rules**, not Maryland state rules.

Maryland campaign finance rules apply to:
- State candidates (Governor, State Legislature, etc.)
- Local candidates (County, Municipal)
- State PACs
- Coordination with state parties on federal races

This document is primarily for:
1. Understanding donor giving patterns at state level
2. Researching prospect history in state races
3. Coordination considerations with state party

## Maryland State Contribution Limits

### Individual Contributions (State Races)

| To | Per 4-Year Cycle |
|----|------------------|
| Gubernatorial Candidate | $6,000 |
| Other Statewide Candidate | $6,000 |
| State Senator | $6,000 |
| Delegate | $6,000 |
| Local Candidate | $6,000 |

### Entity Contributions

| From | Per 4-Year Cycle |
|------|------------------|
| PAC to Candidate | $6,000 |
| Corporation to Candidate | **Prohibited** |
| Union to Candidate | **Prohibited** |
| LLC | [Depends on structure] |

## Maryland State Database

### Searching Maryland Contributions

**Maryland Campaign Finance Database:**
- URL: campaignfinance.maryland.gov
- Search by contributor name, employer, or candidate
- Data available from 2002 forward

### What to Look For

When researching prospects in Maryland database:
- Total state giving (indicates political engagement)
- Candidates supported (party, ideology)
- Timing patterns (early vs. late money)
- Consistency (regular giver vs. sporadic)

---

## Key Differences: Maryland vs. FEC

| Aspect | FEC (Federal) | Maryland (State) |
|--------|---------------|------------------|
| Individual limit | $3,300/election | $6,000/4-year cycle |
| Corporate contributions | Prohibited to candidates | Prohibited to candidates |
| Union contributions | Prohibited to candidates | Prohibited to candidates |
| Disclosure threshold | $200 | $51 |
| Reporting frequency | Quarterly + pre-election | Semi-annual + pre-election |

## Coordination Rules

### State Party Coordination

When campaign coordinates with Maryland Democratic Party:
- Federal rules apply to federal portion
- State rules apply to state portion
- Allocation formulas may apply
- Joint fundraising agreements needed

### Things to Avoid

- Don't assume state contribution limits apply to federal race
- Don't accept corporate/union contributions for federal race
- Don't commingle state and federal funds

## Maryland-Specific Considerations

### Public Financing (State Level)

Maryland has a public financing system for:
- Gubernatorial campaigns (Fair Campaign Financing Fund)

**Note:** No public financing for Congressional races in Maryland.

### Montgomery County (Within MD-14)

Montgomery County has its own campaign finance rules for local races:
- Check county rules for county-level research
- May have different reporting requirements

## Research Applications

### Using Maryland Data for Federal Prospects

When researching prospects for the federal campaign:

1. **Search Maryland database** for state contribution history
   - Indicates political engagement level
   - Shows party/candidate preferences
   - Reveals giving patterns

2. **Compare state and federal giving**
   - Do they give to both? Just state? Just federal?
   - Are state and federal patterns consistent?

3. **Identify state-focused donors**
   - Some donors focus on state races
   - May need different cultivation approach
   - Could be opportunities to expand to federal

### Red Flags in Maryland Research

| Finding | Implication |
|---------|-------------|
| Large state giving, no federal | May prefer state politics |
| Gave to Republican state candidates | Check federal pattern too |
| Inconsistent patterns | Investigate reasons |
| Recent shift in giving | Political evolution? |
