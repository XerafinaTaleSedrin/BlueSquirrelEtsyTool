# Campaign Finance Compliance Framework

Reference guide for FEC and Maryland campaign finance rules applicable to donor research and cultivation.

---

## Federal Election Commission (FEC) Rules

### Contribution Limits (2023-2024 Cycle)

| Donor Type | To Candidate (per election) | Notes |
|------------|---------------------------|-------|
| Individual | $3,300 | Primary and general are separate elections |
| PAC (multicandidate) | $5,000 | Per election |
| PAC (non-multicandidate) | $3,300 | Per election |
| Party Committee (state/local) | $5,000 | Combined limit per election |
| Party Committee (national) | $5,000 | Per election |

**Important:** Primary and general elections are separate, so an individual can give $3,300 for primary + $3,300 for general = $6,600 total per cycle.

### Prohibited Contributions

The following **cannot** contribute to federal campaigns:

- [ ] Foreign nationals (non-US citizens without permanent resident status)
- [ ] Federal government contractors (in their name; employees can give personally)
- [ ] National banks
- [ ] Corporations (directly; PACs are separate)
- [ ] Labor unions (directly; PACs are separate)
- [ ] Contributions in the name of another person

### Contributor Information Requirements

| Contribution Amount | Required Information |
|--------------------|---------------------|
| Under $50 | None required |
| $50 - $199 | Name, address recommended |
| $200 and above | Name, address, occupation, employer **required** |

### Special Situations

**LLCs and Partnerships:**
- Single-member LLC: Treated as individual contribution
- Multi-member LLC: Attributed to members proportionally
- Must verify LLC structure before accepting

**Joint Contributions:**
- Married couples can give separately (each has own limit)
- Joint account contributions attributed 50/50 unless specified

**Reattribution and Redesignation:**
- Excessive contributions must be refunded, reattributed, or redesignated within 60 days
- Campaign must make best efforts to obtain required information

## Maryland Campaign Finance Rules

### State-Level Context

Maryland has separate contribution limits for state and local races. For federal races (like MD-14 Congressional), **FEC rules govern**, but state rules may apply to:

- State party committees involved in federal races
- Coordination with state candidates
- Joint fundraising

### Maryland-Specific Considerations

- Maryland does not have public financing for Congressional races
- State campaign finance database useful for researching donor patterns
- Check for any local regulations in Montgomery County (overlaps MD-14)

## Compliance Checklist for Donor Research

### Before Adding to Prospect List

- [ ] Verify prospect is a US citizen or permanent resident
- [ ] Confirm prospect is not a federal contractor (in their business name)
- [ ] Check if prospect has maxed out to opponent
- [ ] Verify prospect is of legal age (18+)

### Before Recommending an Ask

- [ ] Calculate remaining contribution capacity (max minus already given)
- [ ] Verify occupation and employer are obtainable for $200+ asks
- [ ] Flag any LLC or business entity contributions for verification
- [ ] Note if bundler attribution needed

### For Contributions $200+

- [ ] Obtain full legal name
- [ ] Obtain mailing address
- [ ] Obtain occupation
- [ ] Obtain employer name
- [ ] Document how information was obtained

## FEC Database Research

### Useful FEC Resources

**FEC.gov Individual Contributions:**
- Search by name, employer, or location
- Shows federal contributions for current and past cycles
- Indicates which committee received contribution

**OpenSecrets.org:**
- Aggregated contribution data
- Donor profiles with total giving
- Employer/industry analysis

### What to Look For

1. **Total given this cycle:** Calculate room under limit
2. **Primary vs. general designation:** Which election did they give to?
3. **Pattern of giving:** Early money? Max out? Small recurring?
4. **Other candidates supported:** Ideological profile
5. **PAC contributions:** May indicate different capacity than individual gifts

## Red Flags for Compliance

### Immediate Concerns

- Prospect is non-US citizen without green card
- Prospect is registered federal contractor
- Prospect appears on OFAC sanctions list
- Evidence of straw donor activity
- Contributions in name of business entity without verification

### Requires Additional Verification

- LLC contributions (need to verify structure)
- Large cash contributions (federal limit $100 cash)
- Contributions from family members of foreign nationals
- Rapid contributions from multiple family members (structuring concern)

### Document and Monitor

- Bundled contributions (must track and report bundler)
- In-kind contributions (must value and report)
- Contributions close to the limit (verify running total)

## Reporting Obligations

### Campaign's Reporting Responsibilities

| Report Type | Frequency | What It Covers |
|-------------|-----------|----------------|
| Quarterly | 4x/year | All receipts and disbursements |
| Pre-Primary | Before primary | 20 days before election |
| Pre-General | Before general | 12 days before election |
| Post-Election | After election | 30 days after election |

### 48-Hour Notice

Contributions of $1,000+ received within 20 days of election must be reported within 48 hours.

### Bundler Disclosure

If campaign uses bundlers who aggregate $18,200+ in a covered period, must disclose bundler identity.

## Notes for Research Recommendations

When completing prospect research and recommending ask amounts:

1. Always state the **legal maximum** the prospect can give
2. Calculate **remaining capacity** if they've already contributed this cycle
3. Note if primary or general election designation matters
4. Flag any **compliance concerns** that need verification before solicitation
5. For bundler prospects, note bundler reporting implications
