# Endorsement Scoring Framework

7-dimension evaluation system for prioritizing organizational endorsement targets.

## Scoring Scale

All dimensions use a 1-5 scale:

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Exceptional - Best possible case |
| 4 | Strong - Above average, favorable |
| 3 | Moderate - Average, acceptable |
| 2 | Weak - Below average, concerning |
| 1 | Poor - Significant issues |

---

## Dimensions & Weights

| Dimension | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Organizational Influence | 25% | Credibility, reach, membership size |
| Mission Alignment | 20% | Values and issues match |
| Geographic Relevance | 15% | Presence/membership in MD-14 |
| Endorsement History | 15% | Pattern of endorsing similar candidates |
| Decision Accessibility | 10% | Ability to reach decision-makers |
| Timing/Process | 10% | Endorsement timeline fits campaign |
| Risk Factors | 5% | Reputational concerns, controversy (inverted) |

---

## Dimension Definitions

### 1. Organizational Influence (25%)

The organization's credibility, reach, and ability to move voters or donors.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Major organization; large membership; endorsement is news; mobilizes voters |
| 4 | Significant organization; respected voice; endorsement carries weight |
| 3 | Moderate influence; recognized in their sector; some mobilization capacity |
| 2 | Limited influence; small membership; endorsement has minimal impact |
| 1 | Negligible influence; unknown or discredited organization |

**Indicators:** Membership size, media coverage, past endorsement impact, reputation in sector, social media reach.

### 2. Mission Alignment (20%)

How well the organization's values and priorities align with our candidate.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Perfect alignment; organization exists to advance issues candidate champions |
| 4 | Strong alignment; shared priorities on major issues |
| 3 | Moderate alignment; overlap on some issues, neutral on others |
| 2 | Weak alignment; limited overlap; some conflicting priorities |
| 1 | Misaligned; organization opposes candidate's key positions |

**Indicators:** Mission statement, policy positions, past advocacy, legislative priorities.

### 3. Geographic Relevance (15%)

Organization's presence, membership, and activity in MD-14.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Headquartered in MD-14; large local membership; highly active in district |
| 4 | Strong MD-14 presence; significant local chapter or membership |
| 3 | Maryland-wide organization with MD-14 members; some local activity |
| 2 | National organization with minimal MD-14 specific presence |
| 1 | No meaningful connection to MD-14 or Maryland |

**Indicators:** Office locations, chapter structure, membership distribution, local events/activity.

### 4. Endorsement History (15%)

Organization's pattern of making endorsements in similar races.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Regularly endorses; history of endorsing candidates like ours; endorses in primaries |
| 4 | Regular endorser; generally supports similar candidates |
| 3 | Occasional endorser; mixed history or limited track record |
| 2 | Rare endorser; unpredictable or often endorses different profile |
| 1 | Seldom endorses; or history of endorsing opposing candidates |

**Indicators:** Past endorsement announcements, candidate profiles endorsed, primary vs. general patterns.

### 5. Decision Accessibility (10%)

Our ability to reach and influence the endorsement decision-makers.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Direct relationship with decision-makers; invitation to present |
| 4 | Warm introduction available to key decision-makers |
| 3 | Can identify decision-makers; connection possible with effort |
| 2 | Decision process unclear; limited access to key people |
| 1 | Cannot identify decision-makers; no path to engagement |

**Indicators:** Known contacts, relationship mapping, endorsement process transparency.

### 6. Timing/Process (10%)

How well the organization's endorsement timeline and process fit our campaign.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Timeline perfect; process clear; questionnaire/interview fits our schedule |
| 4 | Good timing; process manageable with some coordination |
| 3 | Timing workable; process requires significant effort but feasible |
| 2 | Challenging timing; process complex or timeline tight |
| 1 | Poor timing; deadline passed or process incompatible |

**Indicators:** Posted endorsement timeline, questionnaire deadlines, interview requirements, announcement timing.

### 7. Risk Factors (5%)

Presence of reputational concerns or controversy. **Note: This dimension is inverted - higher score means LOWER risk.**

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | No risk; universally respected organization |
| 4 | Minor concerns; manageable with awareness |
| 3 | Moderate concerns; some controversy but still net positive |
| 2 | Significant concerns; endorsement may alienate some voters |
| 1 | Major concerns; endorsement could be liability |

**Indicators:** Recent controversies, public perception, association with controversial positions or figures.

---

## Calculating Weighted Score

```
Weighted Score = (OI × 0.25) + (MA × 0.20) + (GR × 0.15) + (EH × 0.15) +
                 (DA × 0.10) + (TP × 0.10) + (RF × 0.05)
```

Where:
- OI = Organizational Influence
- MA = Mission Alignment
- GR = Geographic Relevance
- EH = Endorsement History
- DA = Decision Accessibility
- TP = Timing/Process
- RF = Risk Factors

---

## Priority Classification

| Score Range | Priority | Action |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| 4.0 - 5.0 | **Priority A** | Pursue immediately; intensive cultivation |
| 3.0 - 3.9 | **Priority B** | Pursue this cycle; regular engagement |
| 2.5 - 2.9 | **Priority C** | Opportunistic; engage if resources allow |
| Below 2.5 | **Deprioritize** | Remove from active pursuit |

## Endorsement Categories

Different organization types may have different scoring profiles:

### Unions
- Typically high organizational influence (large membership)
- Clear endorsement processes (often questionnaires + interviews)
- Strong mobilization capacity
- Geographic relevance varies by union type

### Advocacy Organizations
- Influence varies widely by issue area
- Mission alignment is critical factor
- May have specific policy litmus tests
- Endorsement process may be less formal

### Civic Groups
- Often high geographic relevance
- May have nonpartisan restrictions
- Endorsement may be implicit (invitation to speak, etc.)
- Lower mobilization but high credibility

### Elected Officials
- Individual endorsements (not organizational)
- Influence depends on position and popularity
- Geographic relevance usually clear
- May have political considerations for their own races

### Media/Newspapers
- Editorial board endorsements
- High influence but declining reach
- Process is usually editorial board meeting
- Consider both print and digital reach

---

## Confidence Adjustment

Each dimension score should include a confidence level:

- **High Confidence:** Based on verified, multiple sources
- **Medium Confidence:** Based on reasonable inference or single source
- **Low Confidence:** Based on limited data or assumption

For overall scoring, low-confidence dimensions may be weighted less heavily or flagged for additional research.

---

## Strategic Considerations

Beyond scoring, consider:

1. **Sequencing:** Some endorsements open doors to others
2. **Exclusivity:** Some organizations won't endorse if rival org already has
3. **Bundling:** Can multiple related endorsements be pursued together?
4. **Opponent Activity:** Is opponent also pursuing this endorsement?
5. **Announcement Timing:** When would endorsement be most valuable?
