# Relationship Mapping Framework

Methodology for analyzing network connections and identifying optimal paths to donor and endorsement prospects.

---

## Connection Strength Scale

| Level | Relationship Type | Description | Approach |
|-------|------------------|-------------|----------|
| 5 | **Direct/Close** | Personal relationship with candidate or campaign leadership | Direct outreach; personal ask |
| 4 | **Professional/Warm** | Professional relationship; known to campaign through active supporter | Warm introduction; supporter makes connection |
| 3 | **Acquaintance** | Met before; mutual acquaintance can make introduction | Request introduction through mutual contact |
| 2 | **Second-Degree** | No direct connection; connected through someone we know | Find intermediary; build bridge |
| 1 | **Third-Degree/Unknown** | No known connection; would require cold outreach | Cold outreach; find alternate paths |

---

## Mapping Elements

### Primary Connections (Direct to Campaign)

Map direct relationships between prospect and:

- [ ] Candidate personally
- [ ] Campaign manager / senior staff
- [ ] Finance director / fundraising team
- [ ] Key volunteers and organizers
- [ ] Campaign advisory board

### Secondary Connections (Through Supporters)

Identify connections through:

- [ ] Major donors already committed
- [ ] Bundlers and fundraising hosts
- [ ] Endorsers (individuals and organizations)
- [ ] Active volunteers with networks
- [ ] Allied elected officials

### Tertiary Connections (Broader Network)

Consider connections through:

- [ ] Professional networks (industry, associations)
- [ ] Educational networks (alma mater, board service)
- [ ] Community organizations (nonprofits, civic groups)
- [ ] Social networks (clubs, religious organizations)
- [ ] Geographic proximity (neighbors, local business)

## Connection Discovery Process

### Step 1: Identify Known Connections

Start with what's directly known:

```markdown
## Direct Campaign Connections
- [List any known direct relationships]

## Supporter Connections
- [List supporters who may know prospect]
```

### Step 2: Research Prospect's Networks

Identify prospect's affiliations:

- **Professional:** Current employer, past employers, industry associations, board memberships
- **Educational:** Universities attended, alumni associations, ongoing education programs
- **Community:** Nonprofit boards, civic organizations, religious affiliations
- **Social:** Country clubs, professional clubs, social organizations
- **Political:** Past campaign involvement, party activities, PAC affiliations

### Step 3: Cross-Reference with Campaign Network

Compare prospect's affiliations with campaign's network:

```markdown
## Overlap Analysis

| Prospect Affiliation | Campaign Connection | Strength |
|---------------------|---------------------|----------|
| [Affiliation 1] | [Who we know there] | [1-5] |
| [Affiliation 2] | [Who we know there] | [1-5] |
```

### Step 4: Identify Optimal Path

Determine the best introduction route:

1. **Shortest path:** Fewest degrees of separation
2. **Strongest path:** Highest connection strength even if longer
3. **Most appropriate path:** Best fit for the type of ask

## Cultivation Approaches by Connection Strength

### Level 5: Direct Connection

- **Approach:** Personal outreach from connected person
- **Timing:** Can move quickly
- **Ask:** Can be direct
- **Follow-up:** Maintain personal touch

### Level 4: Warm Introduction

- **Approach:** Connector makes introduction, then campaign follows up
- **Timing:** Coordinate with connector's schedule
- **Ask:** After initial relationship building
- **Follow-up:** Thank connector; keep them informed

### Level 3: Acquaintance Introduction

- **Approach:** Request introduction through mutual contact
- **Timing:** May require cultivation of the connector first
- **Ask:** After establishing relationship
- **Follow-up:** Multiple touchpoints before ask

### Level 2: Second-Degree

- **Approach:** Build bridge through intermediary
- **Timing:** Longer cultivation period
- **Ask:** Only after relationship established
- **Follow-up:** Create multiple connection points

### Level 1: Cold Outreach

- **Approach:** Event invitation, policy outreach, or direct contact
- **Timing:** May require multiple attempts
- **Ask:** Much later in cultivation
- **Follow-up:** Persistent but respectful engagement

## Network Mapping for Sectors

### High-Net-Worth Individuals

Common connection points:
- Wealth management firms
- Country/social clubs
- Nonprofit boards (major institutions)
- Industry peer groups
- Educational institution boards

### Business Leaders

Common connection points:
- Industry associations
- Chamber of Commerce
- CEO peer groups (YPO, etc.)
- Business school alumni networks
- Corporate board interlocks

### Professional Service Providers

Common connection points:
- Professional associations (bar, medical, etc.)
- Firm alumni networks
- Industry conferences
- Civic leadership programs
- University advisory boards

### Union Leaders

Common connection points:
- Labor councils
- Political party structures
- Coalition organizations
- Past campaign relationships
- Elected official relationships

### Advocacy Organizations

Common connection points:
- Coalition memberships
- Shared issue campaigns
- Past endorsement relationships
- Staff/board connections
- Funder relationships

## Maintaining the Network Map

### Updates Needed When:

- New supporters are added to campaign
- New endorsements secured
- Events create new connection opportunities
- Staff/volunteer network changes
- New information about prospect networks emerges

### Periodic Review:

- Monthly: Review active prospect connection strategies
- Quarterly: Update master network overview
- Post-event: Map new connections made at fundraisers/events
