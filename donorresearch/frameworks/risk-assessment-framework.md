# Risk Assessment Framework

Systematic approach to identifying and evaluating red flags in donor and endorsement prospects.

---

## Risk Tier Classification

### Tier 1: Immediate Disqualifiers

These factors should stop cultivation immediately and trigger deprioritization.

| Red Flag | Description | Action |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| Criminal Indictment | Pending federal or state charges for relevant offenses | Disqualify |
| Recent Conviction | Convicted of fraud, corruption, campaign finance violations | Disqualify |
| Active Investigation | Known to be under federal/state investigation | Disqualify |
| OFAC Sanctions | On Treasury sanctions list | Disqualify |
| Direct Conflict | Employed by opponent, direct business conflict | Disqualify |
| Public Opposition | Publicly stated opposition to candidate's core positions | Disqualify |
| Foreign National | Non-citizen without permanent resident status | Disqualify |
| Federal Contractor | Prohibited from contributing (if contributing in company name) | Disqualify |

### Tier 2: Proceed with Caution

These factors require careful consideration and may warrant additional review.

| Yellow Flag | Description | Action |
|-------------|-------------|--------|
| Controversial Business | Business practices that have drawn public criticism | Evaluate context; may require senior review |
| Litigation | Pending lawsuits (as defendant) for relevant issues | Research specifics; assess reputational risk |
| Opponent History | Previously supported opponent or opposing party | Assess recency and reasons; may be convertible |
| Divisive Statements | Public statements that could create controversy | Review specifics; assess if association is net negative |
| Industry Sensitivity | Works in industry with reputational challenges | Consider optics; may be fine with proper context |
| Distant Legal Issues | Past legal issues that have been resolved | Note in file; generally proceed if resolved |

### Tier 3: Note and Monitor

These factors should be documented but don't impede cultivation.

| Flag | Description | Action |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Industry Perception | Sector sometimes criticized but prospect not personally | Document; no action needed |
| Controversial Connections | Knows or has worked with controversial figures | Note; don't assume guilt by association |
| Inconsistent Giving | Irregular contribution patterns | May indicate changing views; note for cultivation |
| Political Evolution | Changed party registration or giving patterns | Could be positive (coming our way) or negative |
| Media Mentions | Occasional negative press not related to core issues | Document for awareness |

## Risk Assessment Process

### Step 1: Initial Screening

During T0 validation, check for Tier 1 red flags:

- [ ] Search for name + "indictment" / "charged" / "convicted"
- [ ] Search for name + "investigation" / "subpoena"
- [ ] Check OFAC sanctions list (if high-value prospect)
- [ ] Search for name + "lawsuit" / "sued" (note as Tier 2 if found)
- [ ] Review for public statements on candidate's core issues

### Step 2: Research Phase Findings

During background research, flag any concerns discovered:

- Note the specific concern
- Classify by tier
- Assess relevance to campaign (is this actually a problem?)
- Document sources

### Step 3: Risk Evaluation

For Tier 2 flags, evaluate:

1. **Severity:** How serious is the issue?
2. **Relevance:** Does it relate to campaign issues or values?
3. **Recency:** Is it current or historical?
4. **Resolution:** Has it been addressed or resolved?
5. **Visibility:** How likely is media/opponent to surface it?
6. **Proportionality:** Is the risk worth the potential benefit?

### Step 4: Risk Mitigation

For prospects with manageable risks:

- Document the risk clearly in prospect file
- Brief relevant campaign staff
- Consider timing of engagement (avoid sensitive periods)
- Prepare response if issue surfaces
- Monitor for developments

## Specific Risk Categories

### Financial/Business Risks

| Risk Type | Indicators | Assessment Questions |
|-----------|------------|---------------------|
| Bankruptcy | Court records, news | Personal or business? Recent or resolved? |
| Securities Violations | SEC filings, news | Nature of violation? Settlement or ongoing? |
| Tax Issues | News, court records | Personal or business? Resolved? |
| Business Failure | News, filings | Fraud involved or just business failure? |
| Predatory Practices | News, consumer complaints | Scope and nature? Ongoing? |

### Legal/Ethical Risks

| Risk Type | Indicators | Assessment Questions |
|-----------|------------|---------------------|
| Criminal Matters | Court records, news | Charge type? Conviction? Sentence? |
| Civil Litigation | Court records | Plaintiff or defendant? Subject matter? |
| Regulatory Actions | Agency records, news | Nature? Settlement? Admission? |
| Ethics Violations | News, professional records | Context? Professional sanction? |

### Reputational Risks

| Risk Type | Indicators | Assessment Questions |
|-----------|------------|---------------------|
| Controversy | News, social media | Subject matter? Recency? Resolution? |
| Opposition Research | News, political sources | Already surfaced? Likely to surface? |
| Association Risk | Networks, board service | Direct or tangential? Known involvement? |
| Public Statements | Social media, interviews | Context? Consistent with our candidate? |

### Political Risks

| Risk Type | Indicators | Assessment Questions |
|-----------|------------|---------------------|
| Opponent Support | FEC records, endorsements | Amount? Recency? Ongoing relationship? |
| Opposing Positions | Public statements, advocacy | Core or peripheral issue? Changeable? |
| Party Switching | Voter registration, records | Direction? Reasons? |
| Intra-Party Conflict | News, political intel | Relevant to our race? |

## Escalation Protocol

### When to Escalate

Escalate to campaign leadership when:

1. Any Tier 1 red flag is discovered
2. Multiple Tier 2 flags on a high-priority prospect
3. Findings contradict initial assessment significantly
4. Uncertainty about how to classify a risk
5. Prospect has existing relationship with campaign

### Escalation Process

1. Document findings clearly
2. Classify risk tier
3. Provide recommendation with reasoning
4. Present to appropriate decision-maker
5. Document final decision in prospect file

---

## Monitoring

For active prospects with noted risks:

- Set calendar reminder to re-check periodically
- Monitor news for developments
- Update risk assessment if circumstances change
- Brief campaign staff before major engagement milestones
