# T0 Validation Framework

Pre-research blocker checks to ensure a prospect is worth investigating before investing significant research time.

## Purpose

T0 validation identifies immediate disqualifiers before the research phase begins. A failed T0 check means the prospect should be deprioritized or removed from consideration.

---

## Individual Donor Validation Checklist

### Identity & Status
- [ ] **Real Person:** Donor is a real, living person (not deceased, fictional, or mistaken identity)
- [ ] **Reachable:** Donor is locatable and potentially contactable

### Geographic Connection
- [ ] **Maryland Connection:** Donor resides in, works in, or has demonstrated interest in Maryland or MD-14 specifically
- [ ] **US Citizen/Permanent Resident:** Eligible to contribute under FEC rules

### Political Landscape
- [ ] **Not Maxed to Opponent:** Donor hasn't given maximum contribution to primary or general election opponent
- [ ] **Not Actively Opposed:** No public statements opposing our candidate or core positions
- [ ] **No Employment Conflict:** Not employed by lobbying firm, organization, or industry that creates direct conflict

### Legal & Ethical
- [ ] **No Criminal Issues:** No pending indictments or recent convictions for relevant offenses
- [ ] **No Sanctions:** Not on OFAC sanctions list or similar prohibited parties
- [ ] **No Investigation:** Not currently under federal/state investigation that would create liability

### Practical Viability
- [ ] **Gives Politically:** Has history of political contributions OR other indicators of willingness to give
- [ ] **Accessible:** Some path exists to reach this person (not completely unreachable)

---

## Organizational Endorsement Validation Checklist

### Organization Status
- [ ] **Active Organization:** Organization is currently active and functioning
- [ ] **Makes Endorsements:** Organization has a practice of making political endorsements
- [ ] **Endorsement Process Open:** Organization's endorsement process is accessible (not closed/internal only)

### Political Landscape
- [ ] **Not Endorsed Opponent:** Organization hasn't already endorsed our opponent
- [ ] **No Fundamental Conflict:** Organization's core mission doesn't conflict with candidate's positions
- [ ] **Relevant Races:** Organization endorses in Congressional races (not just local/state)

### Practical Viability
- [ ] **Decision-Makers Identifiable:** We can identify who makes endorsement decisions
- [ ] **Timeline Viable:** Organization's endorsement timeline fits our campaign schedule
- [ ] **Geographic Relevance:** Organization has presence, membership, or interest in MD-14

---

## Validation Results

### Pass (All Boxes Checked)
- Proceed to Phase 1 research
- Create research plan
- Document any notes or concerns for research phase

### Conditional Pass (Minor Concerns)
- Proceed with caution
- Flag specific concerns for deeper research
- May require additional verification during research

### Fail (One or More Blockers)
- Document reason for failure
- Add to deprioritized list with explanation
- May revisit if circumstances change

## Common Blockers

### Donor Blockers Frequently Encountered
1. **Maxed Out:** Already gave maximum to opponent
2. **Wrong Geography:** No Maryland connection whatsoever
3. **Opposed Position:** Publicly supports position antithetical to candidate
4. **Conflict of Interest:** Works for opposing campaign, lobbying firm with conflicts
5. **Legal Issues:** Pending criminal matters

### Endorsement Blockers Frequently Encountered
1. **Already Endorsed:** Committed to opponent
2. **Doesn't Endorse:** Organization doesn't make political endorsements
3. **Wrong Level:** Only endorses state/local, not federal
4. **Mission Conflict:** Core purpose conflicts with candidate platform
5. **Closed Process:** Endorsement decisions made internally, not accepting requests

---

## Re-validation Triggers

A prospect should be re-validated if:
- Significant time has passed since initial validation (>3 months)
- New information emerges that affects validation criteria
- Circumstances change (opponent drops out, legal issues resolved, etc.)
- Research phase uncovers concerning information
